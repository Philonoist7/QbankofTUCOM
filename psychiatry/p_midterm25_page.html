Psychiatry Questions

1. Which of the following is a positive symptom of schizophrenia?

  • A. Hallucination
  • B. Delusion
  • C. A&b
  • D. Social isolation
  • E. Anhedonia

Correct answer: C
a. Hallucination: Correct. Hallucinations (e.g., auditory, visual) are perceptions experienced without external stimuli and are considered positive symptoms of schizophrenia.
b. Delusion: Correct. Delusions are fixed, false beliefs that are not amenable to change in light of conflicting evidence and are considered positive symptoms of schizophrenia.
c. A&b: Correct. Since both hallucinations (A) and delusions (B) are positive symptoms of schizophrenia, this option is the most comprehensive correct answer.
d. Social isolation: Incorrect. Social isolation is typically considered a negative symptom of schizophrenia, reflecting diminished social drive or engagement.
e. Anhedonia: Incorrect. Anhedonia, the inability to experience pleasure, is a negative symptom of schizophrenia.

2. The primary treatment for schizophrenia involves:

  • A. Antidepressants
  • B. Antipsychotic medications
  • C. Benzodiazepines
  • D. Stimulants
  • E. Anticonvulsants

Correct answer: B
a. Antidepressants: Incorrect. Antidepressants may be used adjunctively for comorbid depression in schizophrenia but are not the primary treatment for psychosis.
b. Antipsychotic medications: Correct. Antipsychotic medications are the cornerstone and primary pharmacological treatment for schizophrenia, targeting positive symptoms and helping prevent relapse.
c. Benzodiazepines: Incorrect. Benzodiazepines may be used for agitation or anxiety in schizophrenia but are not the primary treatment.
d. Stimulants: Incorrect. Stimulants are generally contraindicated or used with extreme caution in schizophrenia as they can exacerbate psychosis.
e. Anticonvulsants: Incorrect. Some anticonvulsants are used as mood stabilizers or for aggression, but they are not the primary treatment for the core symptoms of schizophrenia.

3. DSM 5 criteria for diagnosing generalized anxiety disorder is

  • A. 2 years
  • B. 6 months
  • C. 3 months
  • D. 1 month
  • E. Non of the above

Correct answer: B
a. 2 years: Incorrect. This duration is too long for the primary diagnostic criterion of GAD.
b. 6 months: Correct. According to DSM-5, the excessive anxiety and worry associated with Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD) must be present more days than not for at least 6 months.
c. 3 months: Incorrect. This duration is too short.
d. 1 month: Incorrect. This duration is too short; 1 month is more aligned with acute stress disorder or criteria for other conditions.
e. Non of the above: Incorrect, as 6 months is the correct duration.

4. Which of the following traits is present in paranoid personality disorder:

  • A. seek for perfectionism
  • B. Rapid shifting of emotion
  • C. unstable relationships
  • D. general mistrust to others
  • E. Non of the above

Correct answer: D
a. seek for perfectionism: Incorrect. Seeking perfectionism is more characteristic of Obsessive-Compulsive Personality Disorder (OCPD).
b. Rapid shifting of emotion: Incorrect. Rapid shifting of emotion (emotional lability) is more characteristic of Borderline Personality Disorder.
c. unstable relationships: Incorrect. Unstable interpersonal relationships are a key feature of Borderline Personality Disorder.
d. general mistrust to others: Correct. Paranoid Personality Disorder is characterized by a pervasive distrust and suspiciousness of others, interpreting their motives as malevolent.
e. Non of the above: Incorrect, as general mistrust is a core trait.

5. The duration treatment of the 1st major depressive episode is

  • A. 2 years
  • B. 09-12 months
  • C. A&B
  • D. all of the above
  • E. non of the above

Correct answer: B
a. 2 years: Incorrect. While maintenance treatment can last for years for recurrent or chronic depression, for a first episode, continuation treatment is typically shorter.
b. 09-12 months: Correct. After achieving remission from a first major depressive episode, continuation pharmacotherapy is typically recommended for 6-12 months (or 4-9 months depending on guidelines) to prevent relapse. 9-12 months falls within this common recommendation.
c. A&B: Incorrect, as 2 years is generally not the standard for a first episode's initial post-remission phase.
d. all of the above: Incorrect.
e. non of the above: Incorrect.

9. No purging (use of laxatives or induced vomiting) in

  • A. Anorexia nervosa
  • B. Bulimia nervosa
  • C. Binge eating disorder
  • D. all of above
  • E. non of above

Correct answer: C

b. Bulimia nervosa: Incorrect. Bulimia nervosa is characterized by recurrent episodes of binge eating followed by inappropriate compensatory behaviors, which often include purging.
c. Binge eating disorder: Correct. Binge Eating Disorder is characterized by recurrent episodes of binge eating without the regular use of inappropriate compensatory behaviors (such as purging, misuse of laxatives, fasting, or excessive exercise).

e. non of above: Incorrect.

12. No psychotic features at all in ?

  • A. Mania
  • B. Schizophrenia
  • C. Hypomania
  • D. A&B
  • E. non of the above

Correct answer: C

b. Schizophrenia: Incorrect. Psychotic features are defining characteristics of schizophrenia.
c. Hypomania: Correct. By definition in DSM-5, a hypomanic episode is not severe enough to cause marked impairment or necessitate hospitalization, and there are no psychotic features. If psychotic features are present, the episode is manic.
d. A&B: Incorrect.
e. non of the above: Incorrect.

13. What are the clinical features of panic disorder?

  • A. tachycardia
  • B. shortness of breath
  • C. Fear of dying
  • D. all of the above
  • E. Non of the above

Correct answer: D
a. tachycardia: Correct. Tachycardia (rapid heart rate or palpitations) is a common symptom of a panic attack, which is the core feature of panic disorder.
b. shortness of breath: Correct. Shortness of breath or a sensation of smothering is a common symptom of a panic attack.
c. Fear of dying: Correct. An intense fear of dying is a common cognitive symptom during a panic attack.
d. all of the above: Correct. All listed symptoms are characteristic features experienced during panic attacks.
e. Non of the above: Incorrect.

14. Which of the following is a hallmark feature of Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)?

  • A. Social deficit
  • B. Low IQ
  • C. Lack of interest in physical appearance
  • D. Obsessive-compulsive traits
  • E. Language impairment

Correct answer: A
a. Social deficit: Correct. Persistent deficits in social communication and social interaction across multiple contexts are core, defining features of Autism Spectrum Disorder.
b. Low IQ: Incorrect. While intellectual disability can co-occur with ASD, it is not a universal or hallmark feature; individuals with ASD can have any level of intellectual ability.
c. Lack of interest in physical appearance: Incorrect. This is not a specific or hallmark feature of ASD.
d. Obsessive-compulsive traits: Incorrect. Restricted, repetitive patterns of behavior, interests, or activities are a core feature of ASD, which can include obsessive-like behaviors, but "social deficit" is more encompassing of the primary diagnostic area.
e. Language impairment: Incorrect. Language impairment can co-occur and is a common specifier in ASD, but it's not universally present, nor is it considered the hallmark feature over social deficits.

15. Which of the following is NOT included in the diagnostic criteria for ASD?

  • A. Persistent deficits in social communication
  • B. Repetitive behaviors or restricted interests
  • C. Language delay should be present
  • D. Symptoms must be present in early developmental period
  • E. Affect functioning

Correct answer: C
a. Persistent deficits in social communication: Incorrect. This is a core diagnostic criterion for ASD (Criterion A in DSM-5).
b. Repetitive behaviors or restricted interests: Incorrect. This is a core diagnostic criterion for ASD (Criterion B in DSM-5).
c. Language delay should be present: Correct. While language delay is common in ASD and can be specified, it is NOT a mandatory criterion for diagnosis. ASD can be diagnosed in individuals with no language delay or even with superior language skills.
d. Symptoms must be present in early developmental period: Incorrect. This is a diagnostic criterion for ASD (Criterion C in DSM-5).
e. Affect functioning: Incorrect. Deficits in social-emotional reciprocity, which includes aspects of affect (e.g., reduced sharing of emotions or affect), are part of Criterion A for ASD. Therefore, impaired affect functioning is implicitly included.

16. The core symptoms of ADHD include:

  • A. Social communication impairment
  • B. Impulsivity, hyperactivity, and inattention
  • C. Repetitive behaviors and restricted interests
  • D. Hallucinations and delusions
  • E. Anxiety and depression

Correct answer: B
a. Social communication impairment: Incorrect. This is a core feature of Autism Spectrum Disorder.
b. Impulsivity, hyperactivity, and inattention: Correct. ADHD is characterized by a persistent pattern of inattention and/or hyperactivity-impulsivity that interferes with functioning or development.
c. Repetitive behaviors and restricted interests: Incorrect. These are core features of Autism Spectrum Disorder.
d. Hallucinations and delusions: Incorrect. These are psychotic symptoms, not core features of ADHD.
e. Anxiety and depression: Incorrect. While these can be comorbid with ADHD, they are not its core defining symptoms.

17. At what age must symptoms of ADHD typically begin for a diagnosis to be made?

  • A. Before the age of 5 years
  • B. Before the age of 12 years
  • C. Before the age of 18 years
  • D. At any age

Correct answer: B
a. Before the age of 5 years: Incorrect. While symptoms often appear early, the DSM-5 criterion is broader.
b. Before the age of 12 years: Correct. According to DSM-5, several inattentive or hyperactive-impulsive symptoms must have been present prior to age 12 years for a diagnosis of ADHD.
c. Before the age of 18 years: Incorrect. This is too late for the onset criterion.
d. At any age: Incorrect. There is a specific age-of-onset criterion.

18. The first-line pharmacological treatment for ADHD is:

  • A. Antidepressants
  • B. Stimulants such as methylphenidate
  • C. Antipsychotics
  • D. Benzodiazepines
  • E. Antiepileptics

Correct answer: B
a. Antidepressants: Incorrect. Some non-stimulant medications like atomoxetine (an NRI) or certain antidepressants (e.g., bupropion, TCAs) are second-line or alternative treatments but not typically first-line over stimulants.
b. Stimulants such as methylphenidate: Correct. Stimulant medications (e.g., methylphenidate, amphetamine derivatives) are widely recognized as the most effective and first-line pharmacological treatment for ADHD in most age groups.
c. Antipsychotics: Incorrect. Antipsychotics are not used for core ADHD symptoms; they might be used for severe aggression or comorbid conditions.
d. Benzodiazepines: Incorrect. Benzodiazepines are anxiolytics and not used to treat core ADHD symptoms; they can worsen inattention.
e. Antiepileptics: Incorrect. Antiepileptics are not a treatment for ADHD.

19. Adaptive functioning deficits in Intellectual Disability include difficulties in:

  • A. Social, conceptual, and practical domains
  • B. Only cognitive domains
  • C. Only academic performance
  • D. Emotional regulation
  • E. Non of the above

Correct answer: A
a. Social, conceptual, and practical domains: Correct. Intellectual Disability (Intellectual Developmental Disorder) is characterized by deficits in general mental abilities (intellectual functioning) AND impairment in adaptive functioning across three domains: conceptual (e.g., academic skills), social (e.g., social judgment, interpersonal skills), and practical (e.g., personal care, occupational skills).
b. Only cognitive domains: Incorrect. Adaptive functioning deficits are also required.
c. Only academic performance: Incorrect. Academic performance falls under the conceptual domain, but deficits must also be present in social and practical domains, and intellectual functioning itself.
d. Emotional regulation: Incorrect. While emotional regulation difficulties can be present, the core adaptive functioning domains are conceptual, social, and practical.
e. Non of the above: Incorrect.

20. Which of the following is a common feature of OCD?

  • A. Persistent hallucinations
  • B. Intrusive and distressing thoughts
  • C. Persistent mood swings
  • D. Paranoid delusions
  • E. Memory loss

Correct answer: B
a. Persistent hallucinations: Incorrect. Hallucinations are characteristic of psychotic disorders, not OCD.
b. Intrusive and distressing thoughts: Correct. Recurrent and persistent thoughts, urges, or images that are experienced as intrusive and unwanted, and cause marked anxiety or distress (obsessions), are a core feature of OCD.
c. Persistent mood swings: Incorrect. Mood swings are more characteristic of mood disorders (like bipolar disorder) or some personality disorders.
d. Paranoid delusions: Incorrect. Delusions are characteristic of psychotic disorders.
e. Memory loss: Incorrect. Memory loss is not a primary feature of OCD.

21. In OCD, compulsions are:

  • A. Irrational fears
  • B. Physical symptoms of anxiety
  • C. Repetitive behaviors aimed at reducing distress
  • D. Fixed false beliefs
  • E. Sudden mood changes

Correct answer: C
a. Irrational fears: Incorrect. Irrational fears can be part of obsessions or phobias, but compulsions are the responses to them.
b. Physical symptoms of anxiety: Incorrect. Anxiety is often the driver or consequence of obsessions, but compulsions are the acts performed.
c. Repetitive behaviors aimed at reducing distress: Correct. Compulsions are repetitive behaviors (e.g., hand washing, ordering, checking) or mental acts (e.g., praying, counting, repeating words silently) that the individual feels driven to perform in response to an obsession or according to rules that must be applied rigidly, aimed at preventing or reducing anxiety or distress.
d. Fixed false beliefs: Incorrect. These are delusions, characteristic of psychotic disorders.
e. Sudden mood changes: Incorrect. Not a defining feature of compulsions.

22. Which of the following criteria is NOT part of diagnosing substance use disorder according to DSM-5?

  • A. Tolerance
  • B. Withdrawal symptoms
  • C. Continued use despite health consequences
  • D. Experiencing euphoria
  • E. Spending significant time obtaining the substance

Correct answer: D
a. Tolerance: Incorrect. Tolerance is a pharmacological criterion for SUD in DSM-5.
b. Withdrawal symptoms: Incorrect. Withdrawal is a pharmacological criterion for SUD in DSM-5.
c. Continued use despite health consequences: Incorrect. This falls under the "risky use" or "impaired control" criteria clusters for SUD.
d. Experiencing euphoria: Correct. While many substances produce euphoria, the subjective experience of euphoria itself is not a diagnostic criterion for a Substance Use Disorder. The criteria focus on the problematic pattern of use and its consequences.
e. Spending significant time obtaining the substance: Incorrect. A great deal of time spent in activities necessary to obtain the substance, use the substance, or recover from its effects is a criterion under "impaired control."

23. Which of the following is the most common sleep disorder in the general population?

  • B. Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA)
  • C. Insomnia
  • D. Restless Legs Syndrome (RLS)
  • E. Parasomnias

Correct answer: C
a. Narcolepsy: Incorrect. Narcolepsy is relatively rare.
b. Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA): Incorrect. OSA is common, but insomnia disorder is generally reported as more prevalent in terms of patient complaints.
c. Insomnia: Correct. Insomnia disorder (difficulty initiating or maintaining sleep, or early-morning awakening with inability to return to sleep) is the most prevalent sleep disorder.
d. Restless Legs Syndrome (RLS): Incorrect. RLS is less common than insomnia.
e. Parasomnias: Incorrect. Parasomnias (e.g., sleepwalking, night terrors) are less common than insomnia.

27. Which of the following is a core symptom of major depressive disorder?

  • A. Elevated mood
  • B. Low mood
  • C. Increased energy
  • D. Delusions and hallucinations
  • E. All of the above

Correct answer: B
a. Elevated mood: Incorrect. Elevated mood is characteristic of mania or hypomania.
b. Low mood: Correct. Depressed mood (low mood) most of the day, nearly every day, is one of the two cardinal symptoms required for a diagnosis of major depressive disorder. The other is anhedonia.
c. Increased energy: Incorrect. Decreased energy or fatigue is typical of depression.
d. Delusions and hallucinations: Incorrect. While psychotic features like delusions and hallucinations can occur in severe major depressive disorder, they are not considered the most fundamental core symptom like low mood or anhedonia.
e. All of the above: Incorrect.

28. Which of the following is NOT a common symptom of major depressive disorder?

  • A. Suicidal ideation
  • B. Anhedonia
  • C. Low energy
  • D. High mood
  • E. Feelings of guilt

Correct answer: D
a. Suicidal ideation: Incorrect. Suicidal thoughts or ideation are common symptoms of MDD.
b. Anhedonia: Incorrect. Anhedonia (loss of interest or pleasure) is a core symptom of MDD.
c. Low energy: Incorrect. Fatigue or loss of energy is a common symptom of MDD.
d. High mood: Correct. High mood (elevated or euphoric mood) is characteristic of mania or hypomania, the opposite of what is seen in depression.
e. Feelings of guilt: Incorrect. Feelings of worthlessness or excessive/inappropriate guilt are common symptoms of MDD.

29. Which of the following is a potential complication of untreated depression?

  • A. Enhanced cognitive function
  • B. Improved relationships
  • C. Increased risk of suicide
  • D. Enhanced immune function
  • E. Decreased pain sensitivity

Correct answer: C
a. Enhanced cognitive function: Incorrect. Depression often impairs cognitive functions like concentration and memory.
b. Improved relationships: Incorrect. Depression typically strains relationships.
c. Increased risk of suicide: Correct. Untreated depression is a major risk factor for suicidal ideation, attempts, and completion.
d. Enhanced immune function: Incorrect. Chronic stress and depression are often associated with dysregulation of the immune system, potentially impairing it.
e. Decreased pain sensitivity: Incorrect. Depression can sometimes be associated with increased pain sensitivity or somatic complaints.

30. A 25-year-old woman presents to your clinic complaining of intense fear and anxiety in social situations, especially when she has to speak in front of groups. She worries excessively about being judged or embarrassed, leading her to avoid social gatherings. She has experienced these symptoms for several years. Based on this description, which of the following is the most likely diagnosis?

  • A. Generalized Anxiety Disorder

  • C. Panic Disorder
  • D. Obsessive compulsive disorder

Correct answer: B

The patient's symptoms – intense fear of social situations (especially performance situations like speaking in groups), fear of being judged or embarrassed, avoidance of social gatherings, and chronicity – are classic features of Social Anxiety Disorder.

d. Obsessive compulsive disorder: Incorrect. OCD involves obsessions and compulsions, which are not described.

31. A 35-year-old woman presents with a 2-month history of low mood, anhedonia, fatigue, difficulty concentrating, and early morning awakening. She has lost 5 kg unintentionally and describes feelings of worthlessness. What is the most likely diagnosis?

  • A. Bipolar disorder
  • B. Major depressive episode
  • C. Generalized anxiety disorder
  • D. Adjustment disorder
  • E. panic disorder

Correct answer: B
a. Bipolar disorder: Incorrect. While this could be a depressive episode within bipolar disorder, there's no information about past manic or hypomanic episodes. Based purely on the current symptoms, MDD is most direct.
b. Major depressive episode: Correct. The constellation of symptoms (low mood, anhedonia, fatigue, poor concentration, early morning awakening, weight loss, feelings of worthlessness) for 2 months meets criteria for a major depressive episode.
c. Generalized anxiety disorder: Incorrect. The primary symptoms are depressive, not anxiety-focused, although worry can co-occur.
d. Adjustment disorder: Incorrect. Adjustment disorder involves distress following a stressor that is out of proportion, but the symptom cluster here is robust enough for MDD.
e. panic disorder: Incorrect. No panic attacks are described.

32. Which of the following is a core symptom required for a diagnosis of major depressive episode?

  • A. Anxiety
  • B. Psychomotor agitation
  • C. Depressed mood or loss of interest (anhedonia)
  • D. Poor concentration
  • E. sleep disturbance

Correct answer: C
a. Anxiety: Incorrect. While often comorbid, anxiety is not a cardinal symptom required for the diagnosis of MDD.
b. Psychomotor agitation: Incorrect. Psychomotor agitation or retardation is a symptom, but not one of the two essential core symptoms.
c. Depressed mood or loss of interest (anhedonia): Correct. To diagnose a major depressive episode, at least one of these two symptoms (depressed mood or anhedonia) must be present nearly every day for at least two weeks.
d. Poor concentration: Incorrect. Difficulty concentrating is a common symptom but not one of the two essential core symptoms.
e. sleep disturbance: Incorrect. Sleep disturbance (insomnia or hypersomnia) is a common symptom but not one of the two essential core symptoms.

33. A 22-year-old woman presents with sudden onset of palpitations, chest tightness, dizziness, and fear of dying. These episodes last 10-15 minutes and have occurred multiple times. Physical exams and ECG are normal. What is the likely diagnosis?

  • A. Generalized anxiety disorder
  • B. Panic disorder
  • C. Cardiac arrhythmia
  • D. Social phobia
  • E. Obsessive compulsive disorder

Correct answer: B
a. Generalized anxiety disorder: Incorrect. GAD is characterized by chronic, pervasive worry, not discrete, intense episodes like these.
b. Panic disorder: Correct. The description is classic for recurrent panic attacks: sudden onset of intense fear accompanied by physical symptoms (palpitations, chest tightness, dizziness) and cognitive symptoms (fear of dying), with episodes being time-limited. Multiple occurrences suggest Panic Disorder if accompanied by worry about future attacks or maladaptive changes in behavior.
c. Cardiac arrhythmia: Incorrect. While symptoms can mimic cardiac issues, the normal ECG and physical exam make this less likely as the primary psychiatric diagnosis, though cardiac causes should always be ruled out initially.
d. Social phobia: Incorrect. Social phobia involves fear of social situations and scrutiny, not typically spontaneous attacks like these.
e. Obsessive compulsive disorder: Incorrect. OCD involves obsessions and compulsions.

34. 26-year-old man has paranoia, auditory hallucinations, and disorganized speech for 9 months. He believes the government is controlling his thoughts. What is the most likely diagnosis?

  • A. Mania
  • B. Schizophrenia
  • C. Delusional disorder
  • D. Brief psychotic disorder
  • E. Major depressive episode

Correct answer: B
a. Mania: Incorrect. While mania can have psychotic features, the chronicity (9 months) and specific symptom cluster (paranoia, auditory hallucinations, disorganized speech, delusions of control) are more characteristic of schizophrenia.
b. Schizophrenia: Correct. The presence of delusions (paranoia, thought control), hallucinations (auditory), disorganized speech, and a duration of 9 months (well over the 6-month criterion for schizophrenia) with significant dysfunction (implied) points to schizophrenia.
c. Delusional disorder: Incorrect. Delusional disorder involves delusions but typically not prominent hallucinations or disorganized speech, and overall functioning apart from the delusion's impact is not as impaired as in schizophrenia.
d. Brief psychotic disorder: Incorrect. Brief psychotic disorder lasts from 1 day to 1 month.
e. Major depressive episode: Incorrect. While MDE can have psychotic features, the primary symptoms described are psychotic, not mood-related.

35. Which symptom is most a positive psychotic feature of schizophrenia?

  • A. Depressed mood
  • B. Flight of ideas
  • C. Delusion
  • D. Panic attacks
  • E. Non of the above

Correct answer: C
a. Depressed mood: Incorrect. Depressed mood is a mood symptom.
