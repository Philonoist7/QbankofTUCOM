Q9. Dorsum of the nose formed by :

  • A. Septal cartilage
  • B. nasal bones
  • C. Upper lateral cartilage
  • D. all of the above
  • E. Non above

Correct answer: d
a. (Incorrect - partially correct) The dorsal edge of the septal cartilage forms the midline of the lower (cartilaginous) part of the dorsum.
b. (Incorrect - partially correct) The paired nasal bones form the upper (bony) part of the dorsum.
c. (Incorrect - partially correct) The paired upper lateral cartilages form the middle part of the dorsum, between the nasal bones and the lower lateral cartilages.
d. (Correct) The dorsum (bridge) of the nose is a composite structure formed superiorly by the nasal bones, and inferiorly by the upper lateral cartilages and the dorsal border of the septal cartilage.
e. (Incorrect) Option d is correct.

Q10. maxillary sinus drains into:

  • A. Superior meatus

  • E. post nasal space

Correct answer: b

8. Dorsum of the nose is formed by all except:

  • A. Septal cartilage.
  • B. Nasal bones.
  • C. Upper lateral cartilages.
  • D. Vomer.
  • E. Maxillary crest.

Correct answer: D
a. (Incorrect - forms part of dorsum) The dorsal margin of the septal cartilage contributes to the profile of the cartilaginous part of the dorsum.
b. (Incorrect - forms part of dorsum) The paired nasal bones form the bony upper part of the dorsum.
c. (Incorrect - forms part of dorsum) The paired upper lateral cartilages form the middle cartilaginous part of the dorsum.
d. (Correct - is an exception) The vomer is a bone that forms the posteroinferior part of the nasal septum. It does not contribute to the external dorsum of the nose. Clinical Reasoning for choosing D over E if only one answer is allowed: While both D and E are not part of the external dorsum, the vomer is a very distinct, midline septal bone deep within the nasal cavity. The maxillary bone does contribute to the external nose via its frontal process (forming the side of the root of the nose), even though the maxillary crest specifically is an internal feature on the floor. The vomer has no direct contribution to any part of the external nose structure. In many contexts, Vomer is the classic answer for what is not part of the external nasal framework/dorsum.
e. (Incorrect - D is the designated correct answer, though E is also an exception) The maxillary crest (nasal crest of the maxilla) is located on the floor of the nasal cavity and provides attachment for the nasal septum. It does not form the external dorsum of the nose.

Q9. The sphenoid sinus drains into:

  • A. Superior meatus.
  • B. Middle meatus.
  • C. Inferior meatus.
  • D. Sphenoethmoidal recess.
  • E. Post-nasal space.

Correct answer: D
a. (Incorrect) The posterior ethmoidal sinuses drain into the superior meatus.
b. (Incorrect) The frontal, maxillary, and anterior ethmoidal sinuses drain into the middle meatus.
c. (Incorrect) The nasolacrimal duct drains into the inferior meatus.
d. (Correct) The sphenoid sinus drains into the sphenoethmoidal recess, which is a small area located posterosuperior to the superior concha (and thus superior meatus).
e. (Incorrect) The post-nasal space (nasopharynx) is posterior to the nasal cavity, where mucus drains, not a direct opening site for the sphenoid sinus itself.
