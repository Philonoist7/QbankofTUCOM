Obstetrics & Gynaecology Questions

1. Rh disease :

  • A. Occurs when the mother is Rh + ve.
  • B. Occurs when the father is Rhc.
  • C. Occurs when the fetus is Rh +ve
  • D. Can never occurs in the first pregnancy.
  • E. Antibodies are formed against maternal RBCs.

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect. Rh disease (isoimmunization) occurs when the mother is Rh-negative.
b. Incorrect. The father must be Rh-positive for the risk to exist.
c. Correct. The fundamental requirement is that the mother is Rh-negative and the fetus is Rh-positive.
d. Incorrect. It can occur in a first pregnancy if the mother was previously sensitized (e.g., miscarriage or transfusion).
e. Incorrect. Antibodies are formed by the mother against fetal RBCs.

2. All the following tests can be useful in the management of intrauterine growth restriction except :

  • A. Fetal kick chart.
  • B. Cardiotocography CTG non stress test.
  • C. Chorionic villous sampling.
  • D. Biophysical profile.
  • E. Umbilical cord doppler waveforms.

Correct answer: C
a, b, d, e. Incorrect. These are standard methods for monitoring the wellbeing of an IUGR fetus.
c. Correct. CVS is an invasive diagnostic test for karyotyping/genetics, not for the ongoing management or surveillance of growth restriction in the third trimester.

3. The followings are possible complications of IUGR except:

  • A. Intrauterine death.
  • B. Severe hypoxia and fetal distress in labour.
  • C. Meconium aspiration.
  • D. Hypoglycemia.
  • E. Post maturity.

Correct answer: E
a, b, c, d. Incorrect. These are classic complications due to low fetal reserves and stress.
e. Correct. Post-maturity is a risk factor for complications, but it is not a result/complication caused by IUGR.

4. Which is a contraindication of induction of labour:

  • A. History of upper segment caesarean section.
  • B. Severe PE at 37 weeks of gestation.
  • C. Gestational diabetes on insulin at 39 weeks of gestation.
  • D. Postterm pregnancy.
  • E. Chorioamnionitis.

Correct answer: A
a. Correct. An upper segment (classical) C-section scar carries a high risk of uterine rupture and is an absolute contraindication to labor/induction.
b, c, d, e. Incorrect. These are generally valid indications for delivery/induction.

5. Which of the following is an indication for induction of labour:

  • A. Placenta praevia.
  • B. Postterm pregnancy.
  • C. Cord presentation.
  • D. Prior classical C-section.
  • E. Active genital herpes.

Correct answer: B
a, c, d, e. Incorrect. These are indications for Caesarean Section due to safety risks.
b. Correct. Postterm pregnancy is a common indication for induction to prevent post-maturity complications.

6. Complete breech means:

  • A. Flexion at hip joint and extension in knee joint.
  • B. Flexion at hip joint and flexion at knee joint.
  • C. Extension at the hip joint.
  • D. Flexion at the knee joint and extension at hip joint.
  • E. Flexion of one leg at hip joint and extension of the other leg at the hip joint.

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect. This describes Frank breech.
b. Correct. In a Complete breech, the fetus sits cross-legged (hips flexed, knees flexed).
c. Incorrect. Extension at the hip implies a Footling breech.

7. Umbilical cord prolapse is most likely to occur with:

  • A. Frank breech.
  • B. Complete breech.
  • C. Single footling breech.
  • D. All of the above.
  • E. None of the above.

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect. Frank breech fills the pelvis well.
c. Correct. Footling breech is the highest risk because the irregular presenting part allows the cord to slip past.

8. The following drug is used in the treatment of preterm labour:

  • A. Atosiban.
  • B. Pethidine.
  • C. Mefenamic acid.
  • D. Labetolol.
  • E. Calcium gluconate.

Correct answer: A
a. Correct. Atosiban is an oxytocin receptor antagonist used as a tocolytic.
b. Incorrect. Analgesic.
d. Incorrect. Antihypertensive.
e. Incorrect. Antidote for magnesium toxicity.

9. All the followings are risks associated with macrosomia ,except:

  • A. Maternal obesity.
  • B. Prolonged pregnancy.
  • C. Previous large infant.
  • D. Short stature.
  • E. Gestational diabetes melllitus.

Correct answer: D
a, b, c, e. Incorrect. These are well-known risk factors for macrosomia.
d. Correct. Maternal short stature is typically associated with small-for-gestational-age infants, not macrosomia.

10. Diabetes in pregnancy can cause all the following congenital anomalies except:

  • A. Sacral agenesis.
  • B. Central nervous system anomalies.
  • C. Lower limb hypoplasia.
  • D. Congenital heart disease.
  • E. Yellow teeth discoloration.

Correct answer: E
a, b, c, d. Incorrect. Diabetes is teratogenic and causes these defects.
e. Correct. Yellow teeth discoloration is a specific side effect of Tetracycline use, not diabetes.

11. Infants of mothers with GDM have an increased risk of all of the followings except:

  • A. Hypoglycemia.
  • B. Hyperglycemia.
  • C. Hypocalcaemia.
  • D. Hyperbilirubinemia.
  • E. Polycythemia.

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect. They are at risk of hypoglycemia due to fetal hyperinsulinemia.
b. Correct. They do not typically have hyperglycemia; they have hypoglycemia.
c, d, e. Incorrect. These are known complications.

12. Regarding breech presentation,which is true?

  • A. Constitutes 10% of all term deliveries.
  • B. Commmon in postterm labour.
  • C. Vacuum extraction can be used when cervix is fully dilated.
  • D. Forceps can be used for aftercoming head.
  • E. External cephalic version is best performed between 32-34 weeks of gestation.

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect. It is ~3-4% at term.
c. Incorrect. Vacuum is contraindicated for breech.
d. Correct. Forceps can be used to assist the delivery of the aftercoming head.
e. Incorrect. ECV is usually performed closer to term (36-37 weeks).

13. Which of the Following statement is not correct regarding preeclampsia?

  • A. The perinatal mortality is raised.
  • B. Epigastric pain may indicate impending eclampsia.
  • C. There are lowered serum urate levels.
  • D. There may be no fetus.
  • E. The liquor volume may be diminished.

Correct answer: C
c. Correct (as the false statement). In preeclampsia, serum urate levels are raised, not lowered.
a, b, d, e. Incorrect. These are all true statements regarding preeclampsia (D refers to Mole).

14. Which of the following is required for the diagnosis of preeclampsia?

  • A. High urate levels.
  • B. Low plasma magnesium levels.
  • C. More than 24 weeks gestation.
  • D. Odema
  • E. Proteinuria.

Correct answer: E
e. Correct. Preeclampsia is defined by new-onset hypertension plus proteinuria (or end-organ dysfunction).
d. Incorrect. Edema is common in normal pregnancy and is not a diagnostic criterion.

15. Which of the following has not been shown to stimulate (induce) labour:

  • A. Amniotomy.
  • B. Prostaglandins.
  • C. Enamas.
  • D. Breast stimulation.
  • E. Overeating.

Correct answer: E
e. Correct. Overeating has no mechanism to induce labor.
a, b, c, d. Incorrect. These are medical or physical methods known to stimulate uterine activity.

16. Which of the following is true regarding ABO incompatibility between mother and the fetus?

  • A. May affect the first pregnancy.
  • B. Worsens with successive pregnancies.
  • C. Usually causes significant anaemia of the fetus at birth.
  • D. Often requires exchange transfusion.
  • E. Is caused by the Rh(D) antigen.

Correct answer: A
a. Correct. ABO antibodies occur naturally and can affect the first pregnancy.
b. Incorrect. It does not typically worsen significantly.
c, d. Incorrect. It is usually mild.
e. Incorrect. It involves A/B antigens, not Rh.

17. Which of the following drugs are not accepted to be safe for treating pregnancy hypertension?

  • A. Nifedipine.
  • B. Hydralazine.
  • C. Magnesium sulphate.
  • D. Methyl dopa.
  • E. Diuretics.

Correct answer: E
a, b, d. Incorrect. These are standard safe antihypertensives.
c. Incorrect. Used safely for seizure prophylaxis.
e. Correct. Diuretics are generally avoided as they can worsen the intravascular volume depletion seen in preeclampsia.

18. Actions of oxytocin include:

  • A. Anti-diuretic activity.
  • B. Increase in uterine muscle contractility.
  • C. Activation of myoepithelial cells of the breast.
  • D. All of the above.
  • E. None of the above.

Correct answer: D
a, b, c. Incorrect individually. Oxytocin causes all three: water retention (ADH-like), uterine contractions, and milk ejection.

19. Artificial rupture of membrane may cause all except:

  • A. Amniotic fluid embolus.
  • B. IUGR.
  • C. Abruptio placenta.
  • D. Fetal distress.
  • E. Cord prolapse.

Correct answer: B
b. Correct. IUGR is a chronic condition; ARM is an acute intervention during labor.
a, c, d, e. Incorrect. These are potential acute complications of ARM.

20. Which of the following term best describes the renal pathology of preeclampsia?

  • B. Glomerular hypertrophy.
  • C. Glomerular capillary endotheliosis.
  • D. Tubular vacuolization.
  • E. Mesangial cell hypertrophy.

Correct answer: C
c. Correct. Glomerular capillary endotheliosis is the classic renal lesion of preeclampsia.

21. Patient 30 years old presented with badly contolled blood sugar ,shes diagnosed as Gestational D.M on insulin therapy,the most common complications can occur:

  • A. renal failure
  • B. heart failure
