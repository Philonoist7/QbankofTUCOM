General Surgery, Trauma & Anesthesia Questions

1. A 25-year-old woman, weighing 65 Kg, is having a sebaceous cyst excised from her mid back. You are asked to infiltrate local anesthetic around the area before the sebaceous cyst can be removed. Which of the following is the maximum safe dose of lidocaine that can be used before it reaches toxic levels?

  • A. 250 mg
  • B. 200 mg
  • C. 195 mg
  • D. 160 mg
  • E. 120 mg

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: 250 mg exceeds the lower limit of the safe range for plain lidocaine.
b. Incorrect: 200 mg is slightly above the most conservative calculation for the maximum safe dose.
c. Correct: The maximum safe dose of plain lidocaine is 3 mg/kg. For a 65 kg patient, this is 65 * 3 = 195 mg.
d. Incorrect: This is well below the calculated maximum safe dose.
e. Incorrect: This is well below the calculated maximum safe dose.

2. A 72-year-old woman is about to undergo an elective total hip replacement for osteoarthritis. She has a history of hypertension and type 2 diabetes mellitus but no ischemic heart disease or peripheral vascular disease. Which of the following is the most appropriate thromboembolic prophylaxis?

  • A. Intermittent pneumatic calf compression
  • B. Calf-length thromboembolic deterrent elastic stockings and early ambulation
  • C. Full-dose unfractionated heparin to increase the activated partial thromboplastin time to two time control
  • D. Insertion of an inferior vena cava filter
  • E. Subcutaneous low-molecular-weight heparin

Correct answer: E
a. Incorrect: Intermittent pneumatic compression is useful, but chemical prophylaxis is superior for this high-risk surgery.
b. Incorrect: Elastic stockings and ambulation are insufficient as sole prophylaxis for a total hip replacement.
c. Incorrect: Full-dose heparin is for treatment of a DVT/PE, not prophylaxis, due to high bleeding risk.
d. Incorrect: An IVC filter is used for patients with proven DVT/PE who cannot be anticoagulated.
e. Correct: Low-molecular-weight heparin is the standard of care for VTE prophylaxis in major orthopedic surgery.

3. A 22-year-old man (O blood group) sustained a splenic injury in a road traffic accident. He is undergoing a transfusion of 4 units prior to surgery. You are asked to review the patient 10 minutes into the transfusion as he has become unwell and agitated. He has pyrexia (39.5°C) with associated tachycardia (120 beats/min) and hypotension (80/50 mmHg). Which of the following is the most likely cause?

  • A. Non-hemolytic febrile transfusion reaction
  • B. Transfusion-related acute lung injury
  • C. Bacterial contamination
  • D. Air embolus
  • E. Hemolytic transfusion reaction (ABO incompatibility)

Correct answer: E
a. Incorrect: A non-hemolytic febrile reaction does not cause severe hypotension and agitation.
b. Incorrect: TRALI presents primarily with acute respiratory distress.
c. Incorrect: Bacterial contamination can cause shock, but the onset is rarely this rapid (10 minutes).
d. Incorrect: Air embolism presents with sudden cardiovascular collapse, not typically with high fever.
e. Correct: The rapid onset of fever, hypotension, tachycardia, and agitation is classic for an acute hemolytic transfusion reaction.

4. A 35-year-old man is admitted after severing his arm on industrial machinery. His airway is patent and there is no identifiable hindrance to breathing. His pulse is 110 beats/min, blood pressure is 130/105 mmHg, and respiratory rate is 25 breaths/min. Which stage of shock is this patient in?

  • A. Class I
  • B. Class II
  • C. Class III
  • D. Class IV
  • E. Impossible to say from given information

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: Class I shock has minimal changes to vital signs.
b. Correct: The vital signs (HR 100-120, RR 20-30, normal BP with narrowed pulse pressure) fit Class II shock (15-30% blood loss).
c. Incorrect: Class III shock involves a drop in blood pressure and more severe tachycardia/tachypnea.
d. Incorrect: Class IV shock involves profound hypotension and is immediately life-threatening.
e. Incorrect: The information provided is sufficient for classification.

23. A 30 years man admitted to the emergency department after Road traffic accident, chest x.ray show abdominal viscera in the left hemithorax, which is the most likely diagnoses?

24. A 50 years old man presented with pallor and dyspenia on exertion, he has no abdominal pain, he has microcytic hypochromic anemia, the most likely diagnoses is:

  • A. Diverticulosis of colon.
  • B. Peptic ulcer diseases.
  • C. Crohn's diseases.
  • D. Carcinoma of right colon.
  • E. All of the above.

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect: Diverticular bleeding is typically acute and massive, not chronic and occult.
b. Incorrect: While possible, malignancy is the most likely cause to rule out in this demographic.
c. Incorrect: Crohn's disease usually presents with significant abdominal symptoms.
d. Correct: Unexplained iron deficiency anemia in a man over 50 is considered to be from a right-sided colon cancer until proven otherwise.
e. Incorrect: Carcinoma of the right colon is the most probable diagnosis.

25. A 20 years female admitted for elective splenectomy, mostly she has:

  • A. Hereditary neurofibromatosis
  • B. Aplasic anemia.
  • C. Hereditary spherocytosis.
  • D. Pheochromocytoma.
  • E. Hashimoto's disease.

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Splenectomy is not a treatment for neurofibromatosis.
b. Incorrect: Splenectomy is not a treatment for aplastic anemia.
c. Correct: Hereditary spherocytosis is a classic indication for splenectomy to stop the destruction of fragile red blood cells.
d. Incorrect: Pheochromocytoma is a tumor of the adrenal gland.
e. Incorrect: Hashimoto's is an autoimmune disease of the thyroid gland.

26. A 45 years female presented with repeated vomiting due to gastric outlet obstruction, the metabolic changes that may have:

  • A. Hypokalemic hypernatremic metabolic alkalosis.
  • B. Hypochloremic hyponatremic metabolic acidosis.
  • C. Hypchloremic hypokalemic metabolic alkakosis.
  • D. All of the above.
  • E. Non of the above.

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Hypernatremia is not the characteristic finding.
b. Incorrect: Vomiting causes a loss of acid, leading to alkalosis, not acidosis.
c. Correct: Loss of hydrochloric acid (HCl) and potassium (K+) from vomiting leads to hypochloremic, hypokalemic metabolic alkalosis.
d. Incorrect: Not all options are correct.
e. Incorrect: A characteristic metabolic derangement occurs.

28. [All of the following are manifestations of tracheobronchial injury except]

  • A. Pneumothoracic
  • B. Mediastinal emphysema
  • C. Fibrothoracic
  • D. Surgical emphysema
  • E. Hemoptysis

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Pneumothorax is a common acute manifestation due to air leak.
b. Incorrect: Mediastinal emphysema is common from air tracking into the mediastinum.
c. Correct: Fibrothorax is a late complication of conditions like hemothorax or empyema, not an acute sign of airway injury.
d. Incorrect: Surgical (subcutaneous) emphysema is common as air tracks into soft tissues.
e. Incorrect: Hemoptysis is a common sign due to bleeding from the injury site.

6. A 7 years old boy fall on his outstretched hand and presented to emergency room complaining from sever elbow pain, limited elbow movements and swelling, lateral view x ray of injured elbow shows anterior humeral line pass anterior to capitellum. The most common nerve injury in this fracture is:

  • A. Radial nerve
  • B. Musculocutaneous nerve
  • C. Median nerve
  • D. Ulnar nerve
  • E. Posterior interosseous nerve

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Radial nerve injury is less common than median nerve injury in this fracture type.
b. Incorrect: Musculocutaneous nerve injury is very rare in supracondylar fractures.
c. Correct: The x-ray describes a supracondylar humerus fracture. The median nerve (specifically the anterior interosseous branch) is the most commonly injured nerve in this fracture pattern.
d. Incorrect: Ulnar nerve injury is more common in flexion-type fractures.
e. Incorrect: The posterior interosseous nerve is a branch of the radial nerve, more commonly injured in forearm fractures.

7. A 24-year-old man sustains a twisting injury to his knee, with his body turning outwards (the tibia rotates inwards) as he falls while climbing a mountain. His binding fails to release and he feels a crack in his knee. Nothing seems to be out of place, but it swells immediately and he has to be brought down off the mountain on a stretcher. He has a positive Lachman's test. The most likely diagnosis:

  • A. Lateral collateral ligament injury
  • B. Medial collateral ligament injury
  • C. Meniscal injury
  • D. Anterior cruciate ligament injury
  • E. Posterior cruciate ligament injury

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect: LCL injury does not typically cause a large, rapid hemarthrosis.
b. Incorrect: MCL injury is not associated with a positive Lachman's test.
c. Incorrect: Meniscal tears cause a delayed effusion, not immediate swelling.
d. Correct: The mechanism (twisting), "crack," immediate swelling (hemarthrosis), and positive Lachman's test are all classic for an ACL tear.
e. Incorrect: PCL injuries result from a different mechanism and are diagnosed with a posterior drawer test.

14. A 75-year-old female presented to the outpatient department with severe pain in her right hip. We obtained a history of breast cancer that was treated with a mastectomy and chemotherapy five years ago. X-rays revealed lytic Lesion involving the femoral head and neck. Provision diagnosis is

  • A. osteosarcoma
  • B. ewing sarcoma
  • C. secondary tumor
  • D. giant cell tumor
  • E. osteochondroma

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Osteosarcoma is a primary bone cancer of young adults.
b. Incorrect: Ewing sarcoma is a primary bone cancer of children and young adults.

e. Incorrect: Osteochondroma is a benign tumor most common in adolescence.

8. A 56 year old man is involved in a road traffic accident. He is found to have a pelvic fracture. He reports that he has some lower abdominal pain. He has peritonism in the lower abdomen. The nursing staff report that he has not passed any urine. A CT scan shows evidence of free fluid. What is the most likely diagnosis?

  • A. Membranous urethral injury
  • B. Bladder rupture
  • C. Bladder contusion
  • D. Bulbar urethral injury
  • E. Urinary tract infection

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: Urethral injuries cause extraperitoneal urine leakage, not peritonism.
b. Correct: The combination of pelvic fracture, anuria, peritonism, and free intraperitoneal fluid is classic for an intraperitoneal bladder rupture.
c. Incorrect: A bladder contusion would not cause free intraperitoneal fluid or significant peritonism.
d. Incorrect: Bulbar urethral injuries are not typically associated with pelvic fractures.
e. Incorrect: A UTI does not fit the acute traumatic presentation.

10. A 56 year old man presents with lethargy, haematuria and haemoptysis. On examination he is hypertensive and has a right loin mass. A CT scan shows a lesion affecting the upper pole of the right kidney, it has a small cystic centre. Which of the options below is the most likely diagnosis?

e. Incorrect: Polycystic kidney disease presents with multiple bilateral cysts, not a single mass.

11. 2.9 months baby presented with history of accidental exposure of his right hand to very hot soup, followed by sever pain. On examination there was burn wound on dorsum of right hand. The wound was wet, pink with some small blisters. The degree of burn wound is:

  • A. Full thickness burn
  • B. First degree
  • C. Second degree
  • D. Third degree
  • E. Fourth degree

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Full thickness burns are typically white, leathery, and painless.
b. Incorrect: First-degree burns are red and painful but do not have blisters.
c. Correct: A wet, pink, painful wound with blisters is the classic appearance of a second-degree (partial-thickness) burn.
d. Incorrect: Third degree is another term for full-thickness burn.
e. Incorrect: Fourth-degree burns extend into deeper tissues like muscle or bone.

12. A 16-year-old boy presents with left-sided nasal obstruction and recurrent sided epistaxis. A CT scan reveals a mass causing anterior bowing of the posterior maxillary wall. The most likely diagnosis is:

  • A. Adenocarcinoma ethmoid sinus
  • B. Inflammatory nasal polyps
  • C. Juvenile angiofibroma
  • D. Maxillary sinusitis
  • E. Periorbital cellulitis

Correct answer: C

d. Incorrect: Sinusitis is an infection, not a discrete mass causing bony changes.
e. Incorrect: Periorbital cellulitis is an infection of the soft tissues around the eye.
