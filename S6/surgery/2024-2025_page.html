General Surgery & Critical Care Questions

1. A 64-year-old male with severe pancreatitis is transferred to the ICU from an outside hospital. A report is given to the nurse that the patient has received "large-volume resuscitation." Upon reaching the ICU, he is afebrile, tachycardia to 127, and has a BP of 120/60 mmHg. His abdomen is tense and full; he has a Foley in place but no urine in the bag. You suspect abdominal compartment syndrome (ACS). What is the mechanism of his oliguria?

  • A. Extrinsic compression of abdominal organs on the kidneys, leading to reduced GFR
  • B. Elevated renal venous pressure, leading to reduced GFR
  • C. Decreased arterial flow to the kidney, leading to reduced glomerular filtration rate (GFR)
  • D. Extrinsic compression of the ureters, causing an obstructive oliguric renal failure
  • E. Compression of the bladder, causing an obstructive oliguric renal failure

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: This is a vague statement and not the most precise pathophysiological mechanism.
b. Correct: Increased intra-abdominal pressure compresses the renal veins, increasing venous pressure and reducing the glomerular filtration gradient, which causes oliguria.
c. Incorrect: While arterial flow can decrease, the primary mechanism is the direct effect of increased renal venous pressure.
d. Incorrect: Ureteral compression can occur but the predominant mechanism is intra-renal due to venous compression.
e. Incorrect: Bladder compression affects urine storage, not its production at the kidney level.

2. A 22-year-old patient is brought to the emergency department in class III shock following multiple penetrating stab injuries to the torso, chest and abdomen. He undergoes an emergency thoracotomy and laparotomy. In theatre, he requires a total of 30 units of blood. Which of the following is the best statement regarding complications of massive blood transfusion?

  • A. Thrombocytosis is inevitable
  • B. Depletion of factor XI and X is a common problem
  • C. Hypocalcaemia may ensue
  • D. Hyperkalaemia is uncommon
  • E. Hypothermia is rare

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Massive transfusion causes dilutional thrombocytopenia (low platelets), not thrombocytosis.
b. Incorrect: Stored blood is deficient in labile factors V and VIII, not XI and X.
c. Correct: Citrate in stored blood chelates the recipient's calcium, leading to hypocalcemia.
d. Incorrect: Hyperkalemia is a common and serious complication due to potassium leakage from stored red blood cells.
e. Incorrect: Hypothermia is common if blood products are not warmed before transfusion.

4. A 24-year-old woman is scheduled for an elective cholecystectomy. The best method of identifying a potential post-operative bleeding is which of the following?

  • A. Platelet count
  • B. A complete history and physical examination
  • C. Bleeding time
  • D. Lee-White clotting time
  • E. Prothrombin time (PT)

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: A platelet count only assesses for thrombocytopenia and does not screen for other bleeding disorders.
b. Correct: A thorough history (personal/family history of bleeding, medications) is the most sensitive and specific screening tool for potential bleeding disorders.
c. Incorrect: Bleeding time is an outdated and unreliable test.
d. Incorrect: The Lee-White clotting time is an obsolete test.
e. Incorrect: PT only screens the extrinsic and common pathways, missing many other potential bleeding disorders.

5. A 78-year-old woman has a suspected enlarged left groin lymph node. Examination reveals a 2-cm hard, tender and irreducible lump below and lateral to the pubic tubercle with no cough impulse, most likely diagnosis:

  • A. Direct inguinal hernia
  • B. Femoral hernia
  • C. Incisional hernia
  • D. Indirect inguinal hernia
  • E. Spigelian hernia

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: An inguinal hernia is superior and medial to the pubic tubercle.
b. Correct: The location inferior and lateral to the pubic tubercle is classic for a femoral hernia.
c. Incorrect: An incisional hernia occurs at a previous surgical site.
d. Incorrect: An indirect inguinal hernia is superior and medial to the pubic tubercle.
e. Incorrect: A Spigelian hernia occurs at the semilunar line, not in the groin.

6. A 45-year-old man complains of burning epigastric pain that wakes him up at night. The pain is relieved by eating or using over-the counter antacids and H2 blockers. Diagnosis is best confirmed by which of the following?

  • A. Urea breath test
  • B. Serum gastrin levels
  • C. Barium meal examination
  • D. Upper endoscopy
  • E. Upper endoscopy and biopsy

Correct answer: E
a. Incorrect: This test detects H. pylori but does not visualize the ulcer or rule out malignancy.
b. Incorrect: This is for rare causes like Zollinger-Ellison syndrome, not a first-line test.
c. Incorrect: A barium meal is less sensitive than endoscopy and cannot provide biopsies.
d. Incorrect: Visualization alone is insufficient; biopsy is crucial to rule out malignancy and test for H. pylori.
e. Correct: Endoscopy provides direct visualization, and biopsies are essential to confirm the diagnosis, check for H. pylori, and rule out cancer.

7. A 40-year-old male complains of pain in his right iliac fossa over the past 2 days. His pain in the right iliac fossa was preceded by a bout of sudden onset of severe epigastric and right upper quadrant pain 3 days ago that lasted for a few hours. This initial pain subsided with some antacids, which he has been taking on and off for 'indigestion' for almost 18 months. On examination he is pyrexial (39°C) and very tender and rigid over the right iliac fossa, most likely diagnosis:

  • A. Acute appendicitis
  • B. Carcinoid tumour
  • C. Carcinoma of caecum
  • D. Perforated duodenal ulcer
  • E. Right ureteric colic

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect: The preceding severe epigastric pain is atypical for appendicitis.
b. Incorrect: This tumor would not present with this acute two-phase history.
c. Incorrect: Caecal cancer typically has a more insidious onset.
d. Correct: This is a classic history of a perforated ulcer, with initial chemical peritonitis in the epigastrium followed by fluid tracking to the right iliac fossa, causing a secondary bacterial peritonitis that mimics appendicitis.
e. Incorrect: Ureteric colic does not cause peritonitis with rigidity.

8. A 62-year-old woman underwent a transperitoneal right nephrectomy for carcinoma through a transverse incision 5 days ago. She has not had a bowel action since her operation. Examination reveals a slightly tense distended abdomen with no bowel sounds. Her serum potassium is 2.4 mEq/L, most likely diagnosis:

  • A. Caecal volvulus
  • B. Colonic pseudo-obstruction
  • C. Fecal impaction
  • D. Paralytic ileus
  • E. Sigmoid volvulus

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect: A mechanical obstruction like volvulus would cause high-pitched bowel sounds initially.
b. Incorrect: While possible, paralytic ileus is much more common and fits the picture perfectly.
c. Incorrect: This is less likely to cause a completely silent abdomen in this acute post-op setting.
d. Correct: The combination of recent abdominal surgery, abdominal distension, absent bowel sounds, and severe hypokalemia (a major cause of ileus) makes post-operative paralytic ileus the most likely diagnosis.
e. Incorrect: This is a mechanical obstruction and less likely than ileus in this context.

9. A 25-year-old woman complains of bright-red rectal bleeding for 6 weeks. This is not mixed with her stools and comes on every time she opens her bowels. It started toward the end of her pregnancy and got worse after the birth of her baby 2 months ago. She has no other symptoms, most likely diagnosis:

  • A. Anal fissure
  • B. Fistula-in-ano
  • C. Haemorrhoids
  • D. Pruritis ani
  • E. Squamous cell carcinoma

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: An anal fissure typically causes severe pain on defecation, which is absent here.
b. Incorrect: A fistula usually presents with chronic discharge or an abscess, not primarily bleeding.
c. Correct: Painless, bright red rectal bleeding associated with bowel movements, exacerbated by pregnancy and childbirth, is the classic presentation of hemorrhoids.
d. Incorrect: Pruritus ani is itching and does not cause primary bleeding.
e. Incorrect: Cancer is rare in this age group, and hemorrhoids are far more common given the history.

11. A 35-year-old woman presents with breast pain and lumpiness in both breasts. It is particularly worse before her periods, most likely diagnosis:

  • A. ANDI (Aberration of normal development and involution)
  • B. Breast abscess
  • C. Breast cyst
  • D. Duct ectasia
  • E. Galactocele

Correct answer: A
a. Correct: ANDI, or fibrocystic changes, is characterized by bilateral breast pain and lumpiness that fluctuate with the menstrual cycle, worsening premenstrually.
b. Incorrect: A breast abscess is an acute, unilateral infection with signs of inflammation.
c. Incorrect: While cysts are part of ANDI, the term describes the overall diffuse, cyclical condition.
d. Incorrect: Duct ectasia typically presents with nipple discharge in perimenopausal women.
e. Incorrect: A galactocele is a milk-filled cyst related to lactation.

12. A young woman presents with a firm enlarged goitre with bruit, complains of symptoms suggestive of hyperthyroidism, most likely diagnosis:

  • A. Follicular carcinoma of the thyroid
  • B. Graves' disease.
  • C. Multinodular goitre

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: Follicular carcinoma rarely causes hyperthyroidism and would not have a bruit.
b. Correct: Graves' disease is the most common cause of hyperthyroidism in the young. The combination of a diffuse goiter, hyperthyroid symptoms, and a bruit (due to high blood flow) is pathognomonic.
c. Incorrect: A toxic multinodular goiter typically affects older individuals and presents as a nodular, irregular gland.

13. A 64-year-old man presents with a unilateral swelling below the left ear lobe. It is 2 cm in size and stable with no skin changes, pain, or facial nerve involvement, most likely diagnosis:

  • A. Bacterial parotitis
  • B. Mucus retention cyst
  • C. Parotid gland cancer
  • D. Pleomorphic adenoma
  • E. Salivary calculus

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect: Bacterial parotitis is an acute, painful infection.
b. Incorrect: Pleomorphic adenoma is far more common in the parotid gland.
c. Incorrect: While possible, the lack of pain or facial nerve palsy makes a benign tumor more likely.
d. Correct: A slow-growing, painless, firm mass in the parotid gland without facial nerve involvement is the classic presentation of a pleomorphic adenoma, the most common salivary gland tumor.
e. Incorrect: A salivary calculus typically causes intermittent pain and swelling related to meals.

45. 40 years old male presented with gradually increased abdominal pain, associated with abdominal distention, nausea, 2 times of vomiting and constipation. He had history of anterior abdominal wall hernial repair surgery before 2 years. The most likely diagnosis is:

  • A. Bowel perforation.
  • B. Cecal volvulus.
  • C. Intestinal obstruction.
  • D. Acute appendicitis.
  • E. Crohns disease.

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Perforation presents with acute, severe pain and peritonitis, not a gradual onset.
b. Incorrect: While a cause of obstruction, post-surgical adhesions are far more common.
c. Correct: The cardinal symptoms (pain, distention, vomiting, constipation) in a patient with a history of abdominal surgery strongly suggest intestinal obstruction due to adhesions.
d. Incorrect: This presentation is not typical for acute appendicitis.
e. Incorrect: Adhesions are a more likely cause than a Crohn's stricture in this context.

49. 6 years old girl got sever full thickness burn wound on her hand and right thigh after exposure to fire accident. Her parents mentioned that she was comatose(deeply unconscious) immediately after the accident, but regained her consciousness gradually when discharged from their fired house. The caused of coma is:

  • A. Carbon monoxide poisoning.

  • C. Head injury.

e. Incorrect: An allergy is not a plausible cause.

  • A. Flail chest
  • B. Cardiac tamponade
  • C. Tension pneumothorax
  • D. Haemothorax
  • E. Pulmonary contusion

Correct answer: E
a. Incorrect: Flail chest is a life-threatening injury identified in the primary survey.
b. Incorrect: Cardiac tamponade is a life-threatening injury identified in the primary survey.
c. Incorrect: Tension pneumothorax is a life-threatening injury identified in the primary survey.
d. Incorrect: Massive haemothorax is a life-threatening injury identified in the primary survey.
e. Correct: Pulmonary contusion develops over 24-48 hours and is typically identified during the secondary survey, not as part of the immediate life-threats screen.

14. The 43-year-old cyclist collided with a car and received an apparent head injury and trauma. The CT scan shows a grade 4 spleen injury and liver laceration; his HR is 140, BP is 83/52 and RR is 28. What is your management strategy?

  • A. Resuscitate with blood products and, if responding, refer to neurosurgery
  • B. Trauma laparotomy
  • C. Refer to interventional radiology for embolisation of the splenic artery
  • D. Fluid resuscitation with crystalloids and, if responding, refer to neurosurgery
  • E. Refer to interventional radiology for embolisation of bleeding point at liver and splenic artery

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: This ignores the definitive need to stop the intra-abdominal bleeding.
b. Correct: A hemodynamically unstable patient with evidence of significant intra-abdominal bleeding requires an immediate emergency trauma laparotomy to control the hemorrhage.
c. Incorrect: Embolization is for hemodynamically stable patients.
d. Incorrect: Resuscitation should be with blood, not just crystalloids, and surgery is needed.
e. Incorrect: Embolization is contraindicated in an unstable patient.

15. A young man with chest, abdomen, and lower limb trauma was brought to the emergency department. Initial assessment and treatment should include all except:

  • A. securing the airway,
  • B. stabilizing the cervical spine
  • C. assessing breathing
  • D. assessing hemodynamic status
  • E. Fractures fixation

Correct answer: E
a. Incorrect: Airway is 'A' in the primary survey (ABCDE).
b. Incorrect: C-spine stabilization is done with Airway.
c. Incorrect: Breathing is 'B' in the primary survey.
d. Incorrect: Circulation/hemodynamic status is 'C' in the primary survey.
e. Correct: Fracture fixation is part of the secondary survey or definitive care, performed only after all life-threatening injuries (ABCDEs) are addressed.

43. 35 years old male presented to the emergency unit with blunt trauma to the chest on examination, he has surgical emphysema CT scan of the chest reveal tracheobronchial injury. All of the following are manifestations of tracheobronchial injury except

  • A. Pneumothoracic
  • B. Mediastinal emphysema
  • C. Fibrothoracic
  • D. Surgical emphysema
  • E. Hemoptysis

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Air leak from the airway causes pneumothorax.
b. Incorrect: Air can leak into the mediastinum, causing mediastinal emphysema.
c. Correct: A fibrothorax is a late complication of an un-drained hemothorax or empyema and is not an acute sign of airway injury.
d. Incorrect: Air tracking into the soft tissues causes surgical (subcutaneous) emphysema, a classic sign.
e. Incorrect: Injury to the airway causes bleeding and hemoptysis.

44. 23 years old man presented to the emergency department with head trauma after road traffic accident in the ER, patient undergo brain CT scan:

  • A. Epidural hematoma is a biconvex lens shaped can be associated with skull fracture
  • B. Iso to fluid attenuation intracranial bleeding indicate acute state.
  • C. Brain MRI is the investigation of choice in head trauma in the acute setting.
  • D. Epidural hematoma cross suture line.
  • E. Dense artery sign on CT scan indicator of subarachnoid hemorrhage.

Correct answer: A
a. Correct: This statement is accurate. Epidural hematomas are biconvex, arterial in origin, and highly associated with skull fractures.

d. Incorrect: Epidural hematomas are limited by dural attachments at the suture lines and do not cross them.
e. Incorrect: A dense artery sign indicates an intravascular clot (ischemic stroke), not a subarachnoid hemorrhage.

16. A young man presented with leg fracture after twist what will be the shape of the fracture in this condition?

  • A. Spiral fracture
  • B. Comminuted fracture
  • C. Avulsion fracture
  • D. Compression fracture
  • E. Segmental fracture

Correct answer: A
a. Correct: A twisting or rotational force applied to a long bone classically results in a spiral fracture pattern.
b. Incorrect: A comminuted fracture is caused by a high-energy, direct impact.
c. Incorrect: An avulsion fracture occurs when a ligament or tendon pulls off a piece of bone.
d. Incorrect: A compression fracture is caused by an axial loading force.
e. Incorrect: A segmental fracture results from high-energy trauma creating an isolated bone segment.

17. A 25-year-old male with a fractured tibia and fibula from an RTA presents with severe leg swelling. The primary measure to prevent compartment syndrome is?

  • A. Tight back slab above the knee
  • B. Leg elevation to heart level
  • C. Compartment pressure monitoring
  • D. Strong analgesia including narcotics.
  • E. Fasciotomy

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: A tight cast or slab would increase the risk of compartment syndrome.
b. Correct: Elevating the limb to the level of the heart helps reduce swelling by promoting venous and lymphatic drainage, which is a key step in preventing compartment syndrome.
c. Incorrect: Pressure monitoring is a diagnostic tool, not a preventative measure.
d. Incorrect: Analgesia treats pain but can mask the escalating pain that is a key symptom of compartment syndrome.
e. Incorrect: Fasciotomy is the treatment for established compartment syndrome, not a primary prevention method.

19. A young male with a spiral fracture of the distal third of the humerus presented with a wrist drop, indicating a possible injury to ?

  • A. Brachial artery
  • B. Median nerve
  • C. Axillary nerve
  • D. Radial nerve
  • E. Ulnar. Nerve.

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect: Brachial artery injury would cause signs of ischemia (pallor, pulselessness).

d. Correct: The radial nerve runs in the spiral groove of the humerus and is vulnerable to injury with distal third fractures, causing weakness of the wrist extensors and resulting in "wrist drop."

20. A 10-year-old child sustained a greenstick fracture of both forearm bones after falling from a monkey bar. The most appropriate treatment is?

  • A. Open reduction internal fixation
  • B. Closed reduction and casting
  • C. Elastic nailing
  • D. External fixation
  • E. Functional bracing

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: ORIF is rarely needed for pediatric greenstick fractures.
b. Correct: Greenstick fractures are incomplete and inherently stable. They are managed effectively with closed reduction (if needed for alignment) and immobilization in a cast.
c. Incorrect: Nailing is for more unstable pediatric fractures.
d. Incorrect: External fixation is for severe open fractures.
e. Incorrect: Bracing is used later in healing, not as a primary treatment.

21. An 18-year-old female presented with a non-displaced scaphoid waist fracture after a fall, initial treatment is?

  • B. Open reduction and internal fixation
  • C. Thumb spica cast.

  • E. Wrsit splint

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Pinning is a surgical option, not initial treatment for non-displaced fractures.

d. Incorrect: Screw fixation is a surgical alternative, not the initial conservative treatment.
e. Incorrect: A simple wrist splint does not adequately immobilize the thumb, which is crucial.

22. A young man had severe back pain and right lower limb weakness after a traffic accident. The X-ray revealed D12-L1 fracture dislocation. The definitive treatment is

23. Young male pedestrian hit by a car presented to the emergency department with deformity and swelling in the pelvic area. X-ray showed a pelvic fracture with both hemipelvis widely separated. This fracture called:

  • A. Open book fracture

29. A young man fell from 3 meters onto his feet. An X-ray showed a displaced calcaneal fracture that required surgery. The best time for the surgery is when:

  • A. the fracture heals.
  • B. the swelling subsides.
  • C. the patient starts walking
  • D. The pain has ceased.
  • E. Patient Will

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: Surgery is done to facilitate healing, not after it has healed.
b. Correct: Calcaneal fractures cause severe soft tissue swelling. Surgery is delayed until swelling subsides (indicated by the "wrinkle test") to minimize the high risk of wound complications.
c. Incorrect: The patient will be non-weight-bearing before and after surgery.
d. Incorrect: Pain will not cease without treatment.
e. Incorrect: This is not a medical criterion for timing surgery.

  • A. Myelin sheath intact
  • B. Endoneurium injured
  • C. Irreversible
  • D. No Wallerian Degeneration
  • E. Loss of nerve continuity

d. Correct: Neuropraxia is a local conduction block where the axon itself remains intact. Therefore, Wallerian degeneration (breakdown of the axon distal to the injury) does not occur.
e. Incorrect: Loss of continuity defines neurotmesis, the most severe injury.

31. 24 years old female complaining of suprapubic pain, dysuria, frequency and urgency. urinanalysis was done, accordingly antibiotics were given as the patient was diagnosed with UTI. The most dependable test on urinanalysis for UTI diagnosis is:

  • A. RBC
  • B. Pus cells
  • C. Nitrite test.
  • D. Bacteriurea.
  • E. Hyaline cast.

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: RBCs (hematuria) are non-specific.
b. Incorrect: Pus cells (pyuria) indicate inflammation but are not specific for bacterial infection.
c. Correct: The nitrite test is highly specific for UTIs caused by common gram-negative bacteria that convert nitrates to nitrites.
d. Incorrect: While bacteriuria is key, the nitrite test is a more reliable chemical marker on a dipstick.
e. Incorrect: Hyaline casts are non-specific.

32. 72 years old male complaining of frequency, urgency and poor urine stream. Investigation showing total serum PSA=17.2 ng/ml. Which one of the following is not regarded as a provisional diagnosis?

  • A. Prostatitis
  • B. Instrumentation
  • C. Ca.prostate
  • D. BPH
  • E. Prostatic biopsy.

Correct answer: E
a. Incorrect: Prostatitis is a common cause of elevated PSA and urinary symptoms.
b. Incorrect: Recent instrumentation can elevate PSA.
c. Incorrect: Prostate cancer is a primary concern with a high PSA.
d. Incorrect: BPH can cause urinary symptoms and elevate PSA.
e. Correct: A prostatic biopsy is a diagnostic procedure, not a diagnosis itself.

33. 20 years old male had road traffic accident and presented to emergency department with bruise of lower abdomen and scrotal and perineal swelling and blood at the external urethral meatus, after examination and stabilization of vital signs the next step is:

  • A. Foley catheter.
  • B. Suprapubic cystostomy
  • C. Retrograde urethrogram.
  • D. Open surgery.
  • E. Laparascopy.

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Placing a Foley catheter is contraindicated with suspected urethral injury as it can worsen the tear.
b. Incorrect: This is a treatment performed after diagnosis, not the next diagnostic step.
c. Correct: Blood at the meatus is a cardinal sign of urethral injury. The standard next step to confirm and define the injury is a retrograde urethrogram (RUG).
d. Incorrect: Surgery is a treatment, not the next diagnostic step.
e. Incorrect: Laparoscopy has no role in this diagnosis.

34. 25 years old male complaining of a right loin pain with attacks of hematuria and recurrent UTI, after doing investigation including US for him a 12 mm middle calyceal stone was founded. The most preferred treatment option is:

  • C. PCNL
  • D. Flexible Ureteroscopy.
  • E. Pyelolithotomy

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: Watchful waiting is not appropriate for a symptomatic 12 mm stone.
b. Correct: Extracorporeal Shock Wave Lithotripsy (ESWL) is a first-line, non-invasive treatment for renal stones < 20 mm in the renal pelvis or upper/middle calyces.
c. Incorrect: PCNL is reserved for much larger stones (>2 cm).
d. Incorrect: Ureteroscopy is an option, but for a mid-calyceal stone of this size, ESWL is often the preferred first choice.
e. Incorrect: Open surgery is now very rare for this type of stone.

37. newborn male baby had a right scrotal swelling, on examination the swelling can be examined bimanually with negative cough test, the most likely pathology is:

  • E. Testicular torsion.

Correct answer: C

e. Incorrect: Torsion presents as a hard, tender, discolored scrotum.

38. A young male (15years) of age complaining of recurrent epididymal pain and tenderness with attacks of fever and rigor. On examination, the epididymis is tender and indurated and hard, your most provisional diagnosis is:

  • A. Pyelonephritis
  • B. TB
  • C. Epididymal tumor
  • D. ectopic ureter
  • E. Testicular tumor

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: Pyelonephritis is a kidney infection.
b. Correct: Genitourinary tuberculosis (TB) is a classic cause of chronic or recurrent epididymitis, often leading to a hard, indurated, or "beaded" epididymis.
c. Incorrect: Epididymal tumors are extremely rare.
d. Incorrect: An ectopic ureter is a congenital issue; TB is more likely in this age group with these findings.
e. Incorrect: A testicular tumor is a mass in the testis itself, not the epididymis.

  • C. Above 4% strict morphology criteria

Correct answer: B

c. Incorrect: Morphology can be affected, but motility is the most characteristic finding.

e. Incorrect: Liquefaction time is not typically affected by varicocele.

d. Incorrect: Asthma is not a contraindication.
e. Incorrect: Geriatric status is not a contraindication.
