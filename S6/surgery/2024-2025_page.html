General Surgery Questions

1. A 64-year-old male with severe pancreatitis is transferred to the ICU from an outside hospital. A report is given to the nurse that the patient has received "large-volume resuscitation." Upon reaching the ICU, he is afebrile, tachycardia to 127, and has a BP of 120/60 mmHg. His abdomen is tense and full; he has a Foley in place but no urine in the bag. You suspect abdominal compartment syndrome (ACS). What is the mechanism of his oliguria?

  • A. Extrinsic compression of abdominal organs on the kidneys, leading to reduced GFR
  • B. Elevated renal venous pressure, leading to reduced GFR
  • C. Decreased arterial flow to the kidney, leading to reduced glomerular filtration rate (GFR)
  • D. Extrinsic compression of the ureters, causing an obstructive oliguric renal failure
  • E. Compression of the bladder, causing an obstructive oliguric renal failure

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: This is too vague; the primary mechanism is specific vascular compression.
b. Correct: Increased intra-abdominal pressure compresses the renal veins, increasing venous pressure and reducing the net filtration pressure in the glomerulus, which lowers the GFR.
c. Incorrect: While arterial flow can be affected at very high pressures, the effect on venous outflow is the primary mechanism.
d. Incorrect: Ureteral compression can occur but is not the main cause of renal failure in ACS.
e. Incorrect: Bladder compression is used to measure intra-abdominal pressure, not the cause of oliguria.

2. A 22-year-old patient is brought to the emergency department in class III shock following multiple penetrating stab injuries to the torso, chest and abdomen. He undergoes an emergency thoracotomy and laparotomy. In theatre, he requires a total of 30 units of blood. Which of the following is the best statement regarding complications of massive blood transfusion?

  • A. Thrombocytosis is inevitable
  • B. Depletion of factor XI and X is a common problem
  • C. Hypocalcaemia may ensue
  • D. Hyperkalaemia is uncommon
  • E. Hypothermia is rare

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Massive transfusion causes dilutional thrombocytopenia (low platelets), not thrombocytosis.
b. Incorrect: The most depleted clotting factors in stored blood are the labile factors V and VIII.
c. Correct: Citrate, an anticoagulant in stored blood, binds to the patient's calcium, causing hypocalcemia.
d. Incorrect: Hyperkalemia is a common complication as potassium leaks from red blood cells during storage.
e. Incorrect: Hypothermia is very common because blood is stored at cold temperatures and infused rapidly.

3. The mother bring her neonate to emergency with chocking and cyanosis when she feed him for first time with frothy secretion, the diagnosis best made by

  • A. CT scan
  • B. erect abdominal x-ray
  • C. chest x-ray.
  • D. x-ray with nasogastric tube
  • E. Ultrasound

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect: CT scan is not the initial test and involves unnecessary radiation.
b. Incorrect: An abdominal x-ray alone is not diagnostic of esophageal atresia.
c. Incorrect: A chest x-ray alone is insufficient to make the diagnosis.
d. Correct: In esophageal atresia, an NG tube cannot pass into the stomach and will be seen coiled in the upper esophageal pouch on an x-ray, confirming the diagnosis.
e. Incorrect: Ultrasound is not the primary diagnostic tool for this condition.

4. A 24-year-old woman is scheduled for an elective cholecystectomy. The best method of identifying a potential post-operative bleeding is which of the following?

  • A. Platelet count
  • B. A complete history and physical examination
  • C. Bleeding time
  • D. Lee-White clotting time
  • E. Prothrombin time (PT)

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: Lab tests like platelet count are useful but do not replace a thorough history.
b. Correct: A detailed clinical history is the most important screening tool for identifying bleeding risks, such as a personal or family history of bleeding or medication use.
c. Incorrect: Bleeding time is an outdated and unreliable test.
d. Incorrect: The Lee-White clotting time is an obsolete test.
e. Incorrect: PT and aPTT are poor screening tools for the general population as they miss common disorders like von Willebrand disease.

5. A 78-year-old woman has a suspected enlarged left groin lymph node. Examination reveals a 2-cm hard, tender and irreducible lump below and lateral to the pubic tubercle with no cough impulse, most likely diagnosis:

  • A. Direct inguinal hernia
  • B. Femoral hernia
  • C. Incisional hernia
  • D. Indirect inguinal hernia
  • E. Spigelian hernia

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: Inguinal hernias are located superior to the inguinal ligament.
b. Correct: A femoral hernia's neck is located inferior to the inguinal ligament and lateral to the pubic tubercle, matching the description.
c. Incorrect: An incisional hernia occurs at a previous surgical scar.
d. Incorrect: Inguinal hernias are located superior to the inguinal ligament.
e. Incorrect: A Spigelian hernia is located lateral to the rectus muscle, not in the groin.

6. A 45-year-old man complains of burning epigastric pain that wakes him up at night. The pain is relieved by eating or using over-the counter antacids and H2 blockers. Diagnosis is best confirmed by which of the following?

  • A. Urea breath test
  • B. Serum gastrin levels
  • C. Barium meal examination
  • D. Upper endoscopy
  • E. Upper endoscopy and biopsy

Correct answer: E
a. Incorrect: This tests for H. pylori infection but does not visualize the ulcer itself.
b. Incorrect: This is a specific test for Zollinger-Ellison syndrome, not for routine ulcer diagnosis.
c. Incorrect: Barium meal is less sensitive than endoscopy and does not allow for biopsy.
d. Incorrect: While endoscopy is the correct procedure, biopsy is essential for a complete diagnosis.
e. Correct: This is the gold standard, as it allows direct visualization of the ulcer and a biopsy to rule out malignancy and test for H. pylori.

7. A 40-year-old male complains of pain in his right iliac fossa over the past 2 days. His pain in the right iliac fossa was preceded by a bout of sudden onset of severe epigastric and right upper quadrant pain 3 days ago that lasted for a few hours. This initial pain subsided with some antacids, which he has been taking on and off for 'indigestion' for almost 18 months. On examination he is pyrexial (39°C) and very tender and rigid over the right iliac fossa, most likely diagnosis:

  • A. Acute appendicitis
  • B. Carcinoid tumour
  • C. Carcinoma of caecum
  • D. Perforated duodenal ulcer
  • E. Right ureteric colic

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect: Acute appendicitis typically starts with periumbilical pain, not sudden severe epigastric pain.
b. Incorrect: This is not a typical presentation for a carcinoid tumor.
c. Incorrect: The history of severe epigastric pain is not characteristic of a perforated cecal carcinoma.
d. Correct: This is a classic presentation where the initial perforation causes severe epigastric pain, and the gastric contents then track down the right paracolic gutter to cause a secondary peritonitis in the right iliac fossa days later.
e. Incorrect: Ureteric colic presents with loin-to-groin pain and is not associated with this history.

45. 40 years old male presented with gradually increased abdominal pain, associated with abdominal distention, nausea, 2 times of vomiting and constipation. He had history of anterior abdominal wall hernial repair surgery before 2 years. The most likely diagnosis is:

  • A. Bowel perforation.
  • B. Cecal volvulus.
  • C. Intestinal obstruction.
  • D. Acute appendicitis.
  • E. Crohns disease.

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Perforation causes acute peritonitis, not a gradual onset of obstructive symptoms.
b. Incorrect: While a cause of obstruction, cecal volvulus is less common than obstruction from adhesions after surgery.
c. Correct: The patient has the classic signs of intestinal obstruction (pain, distention, vomiting, constipation). A history of abdominal surgery makes post-operative adhesions the most common cause.
d. Incorrect: Appendicitis typically causes localized pain and does not present with complete obstruction.
e. Incorrect: In a patient with prior abdominal surgery, adhesions are a much more likely cause of obstruction than Crohn's disease.

Orthopedics Questions

28. A 40-year-old male runner with a fractured lateral malleolus. If the fracture line is above the syndesmosis, it is classified as:

  • A. Weber D
  • B. Weber C
  • C. Weber A
  • D. Weber B
  • E. Weber E

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: There is no Weber D classification.
b. Correct: A Weber C fracture is located proximal (above) the syndesmosis, implying an unstable syndesmotic injury.
c. Incorrect: A Weber A fracture is located below the syndesmosis.
d. Incorrect: A Weber B fracture is located at the level of the syndesmosis.
e. Incorrect: There is no Weber E classification.

29. A young man fell from 3 meters onto his feet. An X-ray showed a displaced calcaneal fracture that required surgery. The best time for the surgery is when:

  • A. the fracture heals.
  • B. the swelling subsides.
  • C. the patient starts walking
  • D. The pain has ceased.
  • E. Patient Will

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: Surgery must be performed before the fracture heals in a displaced position.
b. Correct: Operating on a calcaneal fracture with significant soft tissue swelling carries a high risk of wound complications. Surgery is delayed until swelling subsides, often indicated by the "wrinkle sign."
c. Incorrect: The patient must remain non-weight-bearing before and for a long period after surgery.
d. Incorrect: The condition of the soft tissues, not the level of pain, dictates surgical timing.
e. Incorrect: Patient will is not the primary determinant for the optimal surgical timing.

30. A young man with foot drop had a history of blunt trauma to the lateral side of his left knee Nerve conduction studies showed common peroneal nerve neuropraxia. Which one is true regarding neuropraxia?

  • A. Myelin sheath intact
  • B. Endoneurium injured
  • C. Irreversible
  • D. No Wallerian Degeneration
  • E. Loss of nerve continuity

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect: Neuropraxia is characterized by localized damage to the myelin sheath; the axon is intact.
b. Incorrect: Injury to the endoneurium defines a more severe injury (axonotmesis).
c. Incorrect: Neuropraxia is a transient, reversible nerve injury with an excellent prognosis.
d. Correct: Since the axon remains intact in neuropraxia, Wallerian degeneration (degeneration of the axon distal to the injury) does not occur.
e. Incorrect: Loss of nerve continuity is the most severe injury, termed neurotmesis.

Urology Questions

31. 24 years old female complaining of suprapubic pain, dysuria, frequency and urgency. urinanalysis was done, accordingly antibiotics were given as the patient was diagnosed with UTI. The most dependable test on urinanalysis for UTI diagnosis is:

  • A. RBC
  • B. Pus cells
  • C. Nitrite test.
  • D. Bacteriurea.
  • E. Hyaline cast.

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: RBCs (hematuria) are non-specific and can be caused by many conditions.
b. Incorrect: Pus cells (pyuria) indicate inflammation but are not specific to bacterial infection.
c. Correct: The nitrite test is highly specific because it detects nitrites produced by common UTI-causing bacteria. A positive result strongly suggests a UTI.
d. Incorrect: Bacteriuria defines a UTI, but on a dipstick, the nitrite test is the most specific indicator for it.
e. Incorrect: Hyaline casts are associated with dehydration, not infection.

32. 72 years old male complaining of frequency, urgency and poor urine stream. Investigation showing total serum PSA=17.2 ng/ml. Which one of the following is not regarded as a provisional diagnosis?

  • A. Prostatitis
  • B. Instrumentation
  • C. Ca.prostate
  • D. BPH
  • E. Prostatic biopsy.

Correct answer: E
a. Incorrect: Prostatitis is a common cause of elevated PSA.
b. Incorrect: Recent urinary instrumentation can cause a significant rise in PSA.
c. Incorrect: Prostate cancer is a primary differential diagnosis with a PSA this high.
d. Incorrect: BPH can cause moderately elevated PSA levels.
e. Correct: A prostatic biopsy is an investigation or procedure used to obtain a diagnosis; it is not a diagnosis itself.

33. 20 years old male had road traffic accident and presented to emergency department with bruising of lower abdomen and scrotal and perineal swelling and blood at the external urethral meatus, after examination and stabilization of vital signs the next step is:

  • A. Foley catheter.
  • B. Suprapubic cystostomy
  • C. Retrograde urethrogram.
  • D. Open surgery.
  • E. Laparascopy.

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Inserting a Foley catheter is contraindicated as it can worsen a urethral injury.
b. Incorrect: This is a treatment that may be performed after diagnosis, not the next diagnostic step.
c. Correct: The signs strongly suggest a urethral injury. A retrograde urethrogram (RUG) is the next step to confirm the diagnosis by visualizing the urethra with contrast.
d. Incorrect: Surgery is a treatment, not the initial diagnostic investigation.
e. Incorrect: Laparoscopy has no role in diagnosing acute urethral injury.

34. 25 years old male complaining of a right loin pain with attacks of hematuria and recurrent UTI, after doing investigation including US for him a 12 mml middle calyceal stone was founded. The most preferred treatment option is:

  • A. Watchful waiting
  • B. ESWL
  • C. PCNL
  • D. Flexible Ureteroscopy.
  • E. Pyelolithotomy

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: A symptomatic 1.2 cm stone requires treatment.
b. Correct: Extracorporeal Shock Wave Lithotripsy (ESWL) is a first-line, non-invasive treatment for renal stones < 2 cm, especially in the middle or upper calyx.
c. Incorrect: PCNL is more invasive and reserved for larger stones (>2 cm).
d. Incorrect: Flexible ureteroscopy is a good alternative, but for a stone of this size and location, ESWL is often the preferred first choice.
e. Incorrect: Open surgery is rarely performed for this type of stone.

35. 65 years old male was diagnosed with BPH and kept on medical treatment. Now the treatment is not effective and surgery is decided for him, which one of the following is not regarded as a surgical modality:

  • A. TURP
  • B. HOLEP
  • C. TUMT
  • D. UROLIFT SYSTEM
  • E. Plasma Vaporization.

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect: TURP is the classic surgical modality for BPH.
b. Incorrect: HoLEP is a modern laser-based surgical modality.
c. Incorrect: TUMT is a minimally invasive surgical therapy that uses heat to ablate tissue.
d. Correct: The Urolift system is a mechanical implant procedure that retracts prostatic lobes rather than resecting or ablating tissue, distinguishing it from other surgical modalities.
e. Incorrect: Plasma Vaporization is a surgical modality that removes prostate tissue.

36. 72 years old male complaining of poor urine stream, drippling, incomplete emptying, hesitancy and haematuria. On U/S patient had a prostate size 150 ml and 3cm vesical stone, the most proper treatment is:

  • A. Watchful Waiting.
  • B. Open surgery
  • C. TURP
  • D. Medical treatment
  • E. Shock wave therapy.

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: The patient has severe symptoms and a large bladder stone, making watchful waiting inappropriate.
b. Correct: The combination of a very large prostate (>100mL) and a large bladder stone is a classic indication for open simple prostatectomy with simultaneous open cystolithotomy.
c. Incorrect: TURP is difficult and risky for a prostate this large, and endoscopic stone removal would make the procedure excessively long.
d. Incorrect: Medical therapy cannot treat the large stone or relieve obstruction from such a large prostate.
e. Incorrect: Shock wave therapy is ineffective for a 3 cm bladder stone and does not address the underlying BPH.

37. A newborn male baby had a right scrotal swelling, on examination the swelling can be examined bimanually with negative cough test, the most likely pathology is:

  • A. Undescended testis.
  • B. Indirect hernia.
  • C. Hydrocele
  • D. Epididymitis
  • E. Testicular torsion.

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: An undescended testis would result in an empty scrotum, not a swelling.
b. Incorrect: While possible, a hydrocele is the most common cause of non-tender scrotal swelling in a newborn.
c. Correct: A non-tender, fluid-filled scrotal swelling in a newborn is most commonly a hydrocele from a patent processus vaginalis.
d. Incorrect: Epididymitis is rare in newborns and would be tender and inflamed.
e. Incorrect: Testicular torsion is a surgical emergency presenting as a hard, tender, discolored testis.

38. A young male (15years) of age complaining of recurrent epididymal pain and tenderness with attacks of fever and rigor. On examination, the epididymis is tender and indurated and hard, your most provisional diagnosis is:

  • A. Pyelonephritis
  • B. TB
  • C. Epididymal tumor
  • D. ectopic ureter
  • E. Testicular tumor

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: Pyelonephritis is a kidney infection, a separate condition.
b. Correct: Genitourinary tuberculosis (TB) classically presents with a chronically hard, indurated, and sometimes "beaded" epididymis, causing recurrent inflammation.
c. Incorrect: Epididymal tumors are very rare and unlikely to cause systemic symptoms like fever and rigors.
d. Incorrect: An ectopic ureter can cause recurrent epididymitis, but the hard, indurated feel is more classic for TB.
e. Incorrect: A testicular tumor is a mass within the testis itself, not the epididymis, and is usually painless.

39. A young couple had a one-year history of primary infertility, after examining the husband a grade two varicocele was founded and therefore seminal fluid analysis was done, what is the most affected parameter:

  • A. Sperm count above 40 million.
  • B. Progressive sperm motility above 20 %
  • C. Above 4% strict morphology criteria
  • D. Above 15 million-sperm\ml concentration
  • E. Liquefaction time 30 minutes.

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: The affected parameter is sperm count/concentration, but motility is more consistently affected.
b. Correct: The most common and sensitive finding on semen analysis in men with a varicocele is decreased sperm motility (asthenozoospermia) due to increased scrotal temperature.
c. Incorrect: Morphology is often affected, but motility is typically the earliest and most significant change.
d. Incorrect: Sperm concentration can be affected, but poor motility is the most consistent finding.
e. Incorrect: Liquefaction time is not typically affected by a varicocele.

40. 25 years old male husband had one-year history of regular sexual relationship now he has an erectile dysfunction of a sudden onset with a nocturnal tumescence the most likely cause of his erectile dysfunction is:

  • A. Neurogenic
  • B. Vasculogenic
  • C. hormonal
  • D. Psychogenic
  • E. Drug effects.

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect: Neurogenic causes do not preserve nocturnal erections.
b. Incorrect: Vasculogenic ED is typically gradual in onset and affects all erections, including nocturnal ones.
c. Incorrect: Hormonal causes are usually associated with a gradual decline in libido and ED.
d. Correct: The combination of sudden onset, a young patient, and preserved nocturnal erections is the classic presentation of psychogenic erectile dysfunction, indicating the physical mechanism is intact.
e. Incorrect: While drugs can cause ED, the triad of features points strongly to a psychogenic cause.

46. 32 years old male presented with hematuria, vomiting and left loin pain, chose the most appropriate answer

  • A. CT with contrast is the investigation of choice
  • B. mid ureteric stone is easily visualized by ultrasound
  • C. calcium containing stones are radiolucent on plain x-ray
  • D. ultrasound may show hydronephrosis and hydroureter
  • E. MRI is the imaging modality of choice

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect: The gold standard for kidney stones is a non-contrast CT.
b. Incorrect: The mid-ureter is difficult to visualize on ultrasound due to overlying bowel gas.
c. Incorrect: Calcium stones are radio-opaque (visible) on plain x-ray; uric acid stones are radiolucent.
d. Correct: Ultrasound is excellent at detecting the secondary signs of an obstructing stone, such as dilation of the kidney (hydronephrosis) and ureter (hydroureter).
e. Incorrect: MRI is not a primary imaging tool for kidney stones.

41. 30 years old female presented with right lower limb pain and swelling 5 days after cesarean section. On examination there is the limb is hot with dark red color of the skin and tenderness of calf region. What is the most appropriate diagnosis?

  • A. Acute limb ischemia
  • B. Cellulitis
  • C. Deep vein thrombosis
  • D. Calf muscle hematoma
  • E. Lymphatic edema

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Acute limb ischemia presents with a pale, cold, pulseless limb.
b. Incorrect: While cellulitis is a possibility, DVT is the most likely diagnosis given the major risk factors (post-surgery, postpartum).
c. Correct: This is a classic presentation of DVT, with major risk factors and signs of unilateral leg swelling, pain, warmth, and erythema.
d. Incorrect: A hematoma is less likely without direct trauma.
e. Incorrect: Lymphedema is a chronic, non-painful swelling, not an acute, inflammatory condition.

43. 35 years old male presented to the emergency unit with blunt trauma to the chest on examination, he has surgical emphysema CT scan of the chest reveal tracheobronchial injury. All of the following are manifestations of tracheobronchial injury except

  • A. Pneumothoracic
  • B. Mediastinal emphysema
  • C. Fibrothoracic
  • D. Surgical emphysema
  • E. Hemoptysis

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Pneumothorax is a very common result of air leaking from the injured airway.
b. Incorrect: Mediastinal emphysema is a classic sign as air escapes into the mediastinum.

d. Incorrect: Surgical (subcutaneous) emphysema is a hallmark sign as air tracks into soft tissues.
e. Incorrect: Hemoptysis is common due to bleeding from the airway tear.

Neurosurgery Questions

44. 23 years old man presented to the emergency department with head trauma after road traffic accident in the ER, patient undergo brain CT scan:

  • A. Epidural hematoma is a biconvex lens shaped can be associated with skull fracture
  • B. Iso to fluid attenuation intracranial bleeding indicate acute state.
  • C. Brain MRI is the investigation of choice in head trauma in the acute setting.
  • D. Epidural hematoma cross suture line.
  • E. Dense artery sign on CT scan indicator of subarachnoid hemorrhage.

Correct answer: A
a. Correct: This is the classic description of an epidural hematoma (EDH)—a biconvex or lens shape, often associated with an overlying skull fracture.
b. Incorrect: Acute bleeding is hyperdense (bright) on CT; isodense bleeding indicates a subacute phase (1-2 weeks old).
c. Incorrect: Non-contrast head CT is the investigation of choice in acute head trauma.
d. Incorrect: Epidural hematomas do not cross suture lines; subdural hematomas do.
e. Incorrect: A dense artery sign indicates thrombosis and ischemic stroke, not subarachnoid hemorrhage.

Eye opening is to painful stimuli, verbal response is confused, yet he flexes his arm to painful stimuli. Glascow coma scale for this patient reveals:

  • E. Non-measurable

d. Incorrect: The assessment is conclusive based on the GCS score.
e. Incorrect: The GCS is measurable from the clinical signs provided.
