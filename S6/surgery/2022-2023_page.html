General Surgery Questions

1. in infantile pyloric stenosis:

  • A. The vomiting is billious.
  • B. Vomiting started from birth
  • C. Treated by antiemetic
  • D. Started in 2nd week after birth
  • E. Not affect weight

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect: The vomiting is non-bilious because the obstruction is proximal to where bile enters the duodenum.
b. Incorrect: Vomiting typically begins between 2 to 8 weeks of life, not immediately at birth.
c. Incorrect: The treatment is surgical (Ramstedt pyloromyotomy), not antiemetics.
d. Correct: The classic onset of symptoms is in the first few weeks of life.
e. Incorrect: Persistent vomiting leads to weight loss or poor weight gain.

2. The treatment of choice for neonates with uncomplicated meconium ileus is:

  • A. Observation.
  • B. Emergency laparotomy, bowel resection, and Bishop-Koop enterostomy.
  • C. Intravenous hydration and a gastrograffin enema.
  • D. Emergency laparotomy, bowel resection, and anastomosis.
  • E. Intravenous antibiotics

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Meconium ileus is a complete bowel obstruction and requires intervention.
b. Incorrect: Surgical intervention is for complicated cases (e.g., perforation, atresia).
c. Correct: A hyperosmolar enema like Gastrografin is both diagnostic and therapeutic for uncomplicated cases.
d. Incorrect: This surgical option is for complicated cases.
e. Incorrect: Antibiotics are not the primary treatment for a mechanical obstruction.

3. Regarding Spina bifida, all are true except:

  • A. All are Neural tube defects.
  • B. Prenatal folate deficiency is an important etiology.
  • C. Anterior variety is asymptomatic.
  • D. Myelomeningocele variety is associated with pelvic organs & lower limbs paralysis.
  • E. Open defects can be treated conservatively

Correct answer: E
a. True: Spina bifida is a type of neural tube defect.
b. True: Folate deficiency is a well-established risk factor.
c. False: The anterior variety can be symptomatic due to compression of organs.
d. True: Myelomeningocele involves the spinal cord and causes severe neurological deficits.
e. False: Open defects require urgent surgical closure to prevent meningitis.

8. Regarding Infection in burn patients.

  • A. A rim of erythema surrounding the wound signals invasive burn wound infection.
  • B. A scheduled rotation of central line insertion sites significantly decreases the rate of catheter line sepsis.
  • C. Selective decontamination of digestive flora reduces systemic infection.
  • D. Gram-positive organisms are the most significant cause of delayed burn wound infection.
  • E. Invasive infection may convert second-degree burn wounds to full-thickness injury and necessitate skin grafting for closure.

Correct answer: E
a. Incorrect: A rim of erythema signifies burn wound cellulitis, not necessarily invasive infection.
b. Incorrect: Evidence does not support scheduled rotation; catheters are changed when clinically indicated.
c. Incorrect: The routine use of selective decontamination is debated.
d. Incorrect: Delayed infections are more commonly caused by Gram-negative organisms and fungi.
e. Correct: This is a classic sign of invasive infection, where partial-thickness burns become full-thickness.

11. Which of the following is true regarding umbilical hernias in adults?

  • A. Most are congenital.
  • B. Repair is contraindicated in patients with cirrhosis.
  • C. Strangulation is less common than in children.
  • D. Small, asymptomatic hernias can be clinically observed.
  • E. Primary closure has recurrence rates similar to those of mesh repair.

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect: Umbilical hernias in adults are typically acquired.
b. Incorrect: Repair is not contraindicated but carries higher risk and requires medical optimization.
c. Incorrect: The risk of strangulation is significantly higher in adults.
d. Correct: Watchful waiting is a reasonable option for very small, asymptomatic hernias.
e. Incorrect: Mesh repair has a significantly lower recurrence rate for defects >1-2 cm.

12. Jaundice with absent urine urobilinogen is most consistent with:

  • A. Hepatitis
  • B. Cirrhosis
  • C. Hemolysis
  • D. Biliary obstruction
  • E. Sepsis

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect: In hepatitis, some bilirubin reaches the gut, so urobilinogen is present.
b. Incorrect: In cirrhosis, some bilirubin reaches the gut, so urobilinogen is present.
c. Incorrect: In hemolysis, there is an excess of urobilinogen in the urine.
d. Correct: With complete biliary obstruction, no bilirubin reaches the gut to be converted to urobilinogen.
e. Incorrect: Sepsis rarely causes the complete obstruction needed for absent urobilinogen.

13. The most common benign tumor of the liver is:

  • A. FNH
  • B. Hepatic adenoma
  • C. Hemangioma
  • D. Mesenchymal hamartoma
  • E. inflammatory pseudotumor

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: FNH is the second most common benign liver tumor.
b. Incorrect: Hepatic adenomas are less common than hemangiomas.
c. Correct: Cavernous hemangioma is the most common benign liver tumor.
d. Incorrect: Mesenchymal hamartoma is a rare tumor of infancy.
e. Incorrect: Inflammatory pseudotumor is a rare, non-neoplastic lesion.

19. Over the past 6 months, a 60-year-old woman with long standing duodenal ulcer disease has been complaining of anorexia, nausea, weight loss and repeated vomiting. She recognizes undigested food in the vomitus. Examination and workup reveal dehydration, hypokalemia, and hypochloremic alkalosis. What is the most likely diagnosis?

  • A. Carcinoma of the fundus
  • B. Penetrating ulcer
  • C. Pyloric obstruction due to cicatricial stenosis of the lumen of the duodenum
  • D. ZES (Zollinger Ellison Syndrome)
  • E. Anorexia nervosa

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Fundal carcinoma does not typically cause outlet obstruction.
b. Incorrect: A penetrating ulcer usually causes a change in pain, not obstruction.
c. Correct: This is a classic presentation of gastric outlet obstruction secondary to chronic peptic ulcer disease.
d. Incorrect: While ZES can cause ulcers that lead to obstruction, the direct diagnosis is the obstruction itself.
e. Incorrect: Anorexia nervosa is an eating disorder and does not cause mechanical obstruction.

Neurosurgery Questions

4. The investigation of choice for assessment of acute intracerebral hemorrhage is

  • A. skull x-ray
  • B. brain MRI
  • C. brain CT scan
  • D. CSF analysis
  • E. EEG

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: Skull x-ray only shows bone, not brain tissue or hemorrhage.
b. Incorrect: MRI is slower and less available in emergencies; CT is the standard.
c. Correct: Non-contrast head CT is fast, available, and highly sensitive for acute blood.
d. Incorrect: CSF analysis is contraindicated due to the risk of brain herniation.
e. Incorrect: EEG measures electrical activity and is used for seizures, not imaging.

5. Regarding Extradural hematoma, the following is NOT true

  • A. Mostly is arterial in origin
  • B. Mostly is venous in origin
  • C. Could be due to dural sinus injury
  • D. Osmotic diuretics are contraindicated
  • E. Considered as a top emergency in head trauma

Correct answer: B
a. True: The classic cause is tearing of the middle meningeal artery.
b. False: This is characteristic of a subdural hematoma; extradural hematomas are overwhelmingly arterial.
c. True: Dural venous sinus injury is a less common cause.
d. False: Osmotic diuretics like mannitol are a key part of emergency medical management.
e. True: It can expand rapidly and requires urgent treatment.

Plastic Surgery Questions

6. Regarding malignant melanoma:

  • A. The incidence is halving every 10 years
  • B. Lentigo maligna melanoma is the commonest clinico-pathological type
  • C. Tumor thickness is the most important prognostic indicator
  • D. Regional lymphadenectomy provides survival benefit in patients with 'thick' tumors
  • E. Acral lentiginous tumors are usually found on the trunk and proximal limbs

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: The incidence has been steadily increasing.
b. Incorrect: Superficial spreading melanoma is the most common type.
c. Correct: Breslow thickness is the single most important prognostic factor.
d. Incorrect: Prophylactic lymphadenectomy has not shown a consistent survival benefit.
e. Incorrect: "Acral" refers to the palms, soles, and under the nails.

7. Regarding keloid scar:

  • A. It is preventable
  • B. It is most common on lower extremities, lower back and abdomen
  • C. Histologically, keloid and hypertrophic scars are no different
  • D. Collagen type I is the predominant type found in keloid scar
  • E. It is limited to the area of wound healing

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect: Keloids cannot be reliably prevented in predisposed individuals.
b. Incorrect: They are most common on the upper chest, shoulders, and earlobes.
c. Incorrect: They are histologically distinct; keloids have thick, haphazard collagen bundles.
d. Correct: Keloids have an overproduction of extracellular matrix, predominantly type I collagen.
e. Incorrect: This describes a hypertrophic scar; keloids grow beyond the original wound boundaries.

Vascular Surgery Questions

9. Regarding arterial injury:

  • A. All arterial injuries are associated with absence of a palpable pulse.
  • B. Preoperative arteriography is required to diagnose an arterial injury.
  • C. The presence of Doppler signals indicates that an arterial injury has not occurred.
  • D. Patients with critical limb ischemia have paralysis and paresthesias.
  • E. In all patients with multiple trauma, arterial injuries should be repaired before other injuries are addressed.

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect: A pulse may still be palpable distal to a non-occlusive injury.
b. Incorrect: CT Angiography is now the standard; patients with hard signs may go directly to surgery.
c. Incorrect: A Doppler signal may be present, but its character (e.g., monophasic) can indicate injury.
d. Correct: Paralysis and paresthesia are classic signs of nerve dysfunction due to critical ischemia.
e. Incorrect: The principle is "life over limb"; life-threatening injuries are addressed first.

10. Regarding gas gangrene:

  • A. It is due to Clostridium botulinum infection
  • B. Clostridial species are gram-negative spore forming anaerobes
  • C. The clinical features are due to the release of protein endotoxin
  • D. Gas is not invariably present in the muscle compartments
  • E. Surgical debridement and antibiotics are an essential part of treatment

Correct answer: E
a. Incorrect: It is primarily caused by Clostridium perfringens.
b. Incorrect: Clostridial species are Gram-positive.
c. Incorrect: The features are due to potent exotoxins, not endotoxins.
d. True but E is better: Gas may be absent early on, but its absence does not rule out the diagnosis.
e. True: Aggressive surgical debridement and high-dose antibiotics are the cornerstones of treatment.

20. What is the initial test to establish the diagnosis of deep venous thrombosis?

  • A. Venography
  • B. Venous duplex ultrasound
  • C. Impedance plethysmography
  • D. Radio-labeled fibrinogen
  • E. Assay of fibrin/fibrinogen products

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: Venography is the historical gold standard but is invasive and rarely used now.
b. Correct: Venous duplex ultrasound is the non-invasive, accurate, and standard initial test of choice.
c. Incorrect: This is an obsolete and less accurate test.
d. Incorrect: This nuclear scan is rarely used for acute DVT diagnosis.
e. Incorrect: This refers to D-dimer, which is used to rule out DVT but not to establish the diagnosis.

Breast Surgery Questions

14. A 36-year-old woman complains of a 3-month history of bloody discharge from the nipple. At examination, a small nodule is found, deep to the areola. Careful palpation of the nipple-areolar complex results in blood appearing at the 3 O'clock position. Mammogram findings are normal. What is the likeliest diagnosis?

  • A. Intraductal papilloma
  • B. Breast cyst
  • C. Intraductal carcinoma
  • D. Carcinoma in situ
  • E. Fat necrosis

Correct answer: A
a. Correct: Solitary intraductal papilloma is the most common cause of spontaneous, unilateral, bloody nipple discharge.
b. Incorrect: Cyst discharge is usually clear, yellow, or greenish, not bloody.
c. Incorrect: Carcinoma is a possible but less common cause than a benign papilloma.
d. Incorrect: Carcinoma in situ is a type of carcinoma; papilloma is more likely.
e. Incorrect: Fat necrosis presents as a mass and does not cause nipple discharge.

15. In Paget's disease of the nipple which of the following is TRUE?

  • A. Carcinoma of the breast is rarely found
  • B. Surgical therapy often fails to cure Paget's disease.
  • C. The diagnosis should be made by nipple biopsy when suspected.
  • D. The underlying carcinoma when present is very large.
  • E. Paget's disease of the bone is commonly encountered.

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: An underlying carcinoma is found in over 95% of cases.
b. Incorrect: Surgical therapy is the definitive and curative treatment.
c. Correct: Any suspicious nipple lesion requires a full-thickness biopsy for diagnosis.
d. Incorrect: The underlying carcinoma can be of any size and is often non-palpable DCIS.
e. Incorrect: Paget's disease of the bone is a completely separate and unrelated disorder.

Thoracic Surgery Questions

16. An asymptomatic 56-year-old man is found on routine chest x-ray to have a 2-cm nodule-central tumor in the upper lobe of the right lung. The lesion is not calcified. No previous x-rays exist. What is the most appropriate initial step toward making a diagnosis?

  • A. Fiberoptic bronchoscopy
  • B. Bone scan
  • C. Thoracotomy
  • D. Observation at follow-up examination in 6 months
  • E. Mediastinoscopy

Correct answer: A
a. Correct: For a central lesion, bronchoscopy is the best initial step to obtain a tissue diagnosis.
b. Incorrect: A bone scan is for staging after a diagnosis of cancer is confirmed.
c. Incorrect: Thoracotomy is a major resection surgery, not a first-line diagnostic step.
d. Incorrect: Observation is inappropriate for a new, suspicious 2-cm nodule in this age group.
e. Incorrect: Mediastinoscopy is for staging lymph nodes, not diagnosing the primary lung lesion.

Head and Neck Surgery Questions

17. What is most characteristic of branchial cleft cysts?

  • A. They usually appear in the axilla.
  • B. They may become infected after an URTI.
  • C. They may be traced to the stomach.
  • D. They arise from endodermal tissue.
  • E. They frequently cause brachial plexus lesions.

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect: They characteristically appear on the lateral side of the neck.
b. Correct: A common presentation is painful swelling precipitated by an upper respiratory tract infection.
c. Incorrect: The tract of the most common type traces to the tonsillar fossa in the pharynx.
d. Incorrect: They are lined by ectodermal derivatives (squamous epithelium).
e. Incorrect: They are located anteriorly and do not typically involve the brachial plexus.

18. What is true of cystic hygroma?

  • A. It arises from sweat glands in the neck.
  • B. It is usually an anterior midline structure.
  • C. It may occur in the mediastinum.
  • D. Its lesions are usually easy to enucleate.
  • E. It is premalignant.

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect: It is a congenital malformation of the lymphatic system (lymphangioma).
b. Incorrect: It is most commonly found in the posterior triangle of the neck.
c. Correct: While most common in the neck, they can extend into or arise in the mediastinum.
d. Incorrect: They are notoriously difficult to excise completely as they infiltrate normal tissues.
e. Incorrect: It is a benign malformation with no malignant potential.
