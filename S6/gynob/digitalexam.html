Gynecology & Obstetrics Questions

1. ...contraceptives on the incidence of DVT and embolism.

  • A. Decreases the incidence.
  • B. doesn't affect the incidence.
  • C. increases thrombosis but not pulmonary embolism.
  • D. increases the incidence.
  • E. increases pulmonary embolism but not DVT.

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect. Combined oral contraceptives (COCs) increase the risk.
b. Incorrect.
c. Incorrect. DVT can lead to PE; both risks are increased.
d. Correct. The estrogen component increases production of clotting factors (VII, X, fibrinogen) and decreases antithrombin III, leading to a hypercoagulable state.
e. Incorrect. PE is usually a sequela of DVT.

2. Regarding ovulation can be diagnosed by the following measures, EXCEPT:

  • A. measuring day 14 serum progesterone.
  • B. Observing a rise in basal body temperature in the 2nd half of the menstrual cycle.
  • C. study of the cervical mucous.
  • D. Endometrial histology.
  • E. Ultrasound.

Correct answer: A
a. Correct (as the Exception). Serum progesterone peaks in the mid-luteal phase (around Day 21), not Day 14 (ovulation day).
b. Incorrect. Progesterone causes a thermogenic effect after ovulation.
c. Incorrect. Cervical mucus changes (like ferning) disappear after ovulation.
d. Incorrect. Secretory changes confirm ovulation.
e. Incorrect. Ultrasound can show follicle collapse and fluid in the Pouch of Douglas.

3. When the cervix protrudes well beyond the introitus, in a case of uterine prolapse, the prolapse is called which of the following:

  • A. 1st degree.
  • B. 2nd degree.
  • C. 3rd degree.
  • D. Procedentia.
  • E. None of the above.

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect. Descent is halfway to the hymen.
b. Incorrect. Descent is to the level of the hymen (introitus).
c. Correct. 3rd degree is when the cervix protrudes beyond the hymen/introitus.
d. Incorrect. Procidentia typically refers to complete eversion/4th degree.
e. Incorrect.

4. Pelvic endometriosis:

  • A. Is a cause of primary dysmenorrhoea.
  • B. Associated with excessive vaginal discharge.
  • C. Associated with prolonged use of IUCD.
  • D. Combined OCP are predisposing factor.
  • E. Can be diagnosed by laparoscopy.

Correct answer: E
a. Incorrect. It causes secondary dysmenorrhea.
b. Incorrect. Discharge suggests infection.
c. Incorrect. No direct causal link.
d. Incorrect. COCs are used to treat endometriosis.
e. Correct. Laparoscopy with biopsy is the gold standard for diagnosis.

5. The commonest site for ovarian [metastasis/spread] is:

  • A. Pouce of Douglus.
  • B. Liver.
  • C. Uterus.
  • D. Peritoneum.
  • E. Bone.

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect. A specific site, but generalized peritoneal spread is the broader mechanism.
b. Incorrect. Less common than peritoneal spread.
c. Incorrect.
d. Correct. Ovarian cancer spreads primarily by exfoliation and transcoelomic (peritoneal) seeding.
e. Incorrect. Rare site.

6. Types of uterine fibroid degeneration include all EXCEPT:

  • A. Red degeneration.
  • B. Yellow degeneration.
  • C. Calcification.
  • D. Hyaline degeneration.
  • E. Fatty degeneration.

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect. Occurs during pregnancy.
b. Correct (as the Exception). "Yellow degeneration" is not a distinct classification; the named types are Hyaline, Red, Calcific, Fatty, etc.
c. Incorrect. Occurs in post-menopausal women.
d. Incorrect. The most common type.
e. Incorrect. A recognized type.

7. Risk factors of cervical cancer include all the following [EXCEPT]:

  • A. Nulliparity.
  • B. Multiple sexual partners.
  • C. History of papilloma virus infection.
  • D. smoking.
  • E. sexual activity at early age.

Correct answer: A
a. Correct (as the Exception). High parity (multiparity) is the risk factor for cervical cancer; nulliparity is a risk for endometrial/ovarian cancer.
b. Incorrect. Increases HPV risk.
c. Incorrect. HPV is the primary cause.
d. Incorrect. A known co-factor.
e. Incorrect. Transformation zone is vulnerable in youth.

8. What is the most frequent site for metastasis from a malignant gestational trophoblastic neoplasms.

  • A. Brain.
  • B. Liver.
  • C. Kidneys.
  • D. Vulva.
  • E. Lung.

Correct answer: E
a. Incorrect. Late complication.
b. Incorrect. Indicates poor prognosis.
c. Incorrect. Less common.
d. Incorrect. Can occur, but not the most frequent.
e. Correct. Spread is hematogenous, and lungs are the first capillary bed encountered (80% of metastases).

9. Choriocarcinomas can occur:

  • A. After abortion
  • B. Spontaneously.
  • C. After Hydatiform mole.
  • D. After normal pregnancy.
  • E. All of the above.

Correct answer: E
a. Incorrect. Approx 25% of cases.
b. Incorrect. Can arise from non-gestational germ cells or undetected loss.
c. Incorrect. The most common antecedent (50%).
d. Incorrect. Approx 25% of cases.
e. Correct. It can follow any type of pregnancy event.

10. Which is a gonadotropin:

  • A. FSH.
  • B. Oestrogen.
  • C. Oxytocin.
  • D. Progesterone.
  • E. Estradiol.

Correct answer: A
a. Correct. Follicle Stimulating Hormone is a pituitary glycoprotein gonadotropin.
b. Incorrect. Steroid hormone.
c. Incorrect. Peptide hormone.
d. Incorrect. Steroid hormone.
e. Incorrect. Steroid hormone.

11. Risk factors for ectopic pregnancy are,except :

  • A. prior ectopic
  • B. history of infertility
  • C. prior tubal surgery
  • D. maternal age of 16-19 years
  • E. history of PID

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect. High recurrence risk.
b. Incorrect. Infertility and ART increase risk.
c. Incorrect. Scarring increases risk.
d. Correct (as the Exception). Risk increases with age (>35), not typically in adolescence.
e. Incorrect. PID causes tubal damage.

12. Of the following,the most common benign tumor of the uterus is :

  • A. leiomyosarcoma
  • B. endometioma
  • C. leiomyoma
  • D. adenomyosis
  • E. krukenburg's

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect. Malignant.
b. Incorrect. Usually ovarian.
c. Correct. Leiomyomas (fibroids) are the most common benign pelvic tumors.
d. Incorrect. Presence of endometrial tissue in myometrium, not a distinct "tumor".
e. Incorrect. Metastatic ovarian tumor.

13. which of the following pubertal event would occur even in the absence of ovarian estrogen production:

  • A. thelarche
  • B. menarche
  • C. pubarche
  • D. skeletal growth
  • E. vaginal cornification

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect. Requires estrogen.
b. Incorrect. Requires estrogen.
c. Correct. Pubarche (pubic hair) is driven by adrenal androgens (adrenarche), independent of ovarian estrogen.
d. Incorrect. Estrogen drives growth spurt/fusion.
e. Incorrect. Direct estrogen effect.

14. in ovarian cyst all true except:

  • A. may cause pain
  • B. can be functional
  • C. discovered by US
  • D. may reach 10cm
  • E. always treated by surgery

Correct answer: E
a. Incorrect. Can cause pain via distension or torsion.
b. Incorrect. Follicular/corpus luteum cysts are functional.
c. Incorrect. US is primary imaging.
d. Incorrect. Can be large.
e. Correct (as the Exception). Many resolve spontaneously and are managed by observation.

15. all the following symptoms are consistent with uterine fibroids except:

  • A. nausea & vomiting
  • B. menorrhagia
  • C. backpain
  • D. urinary frequency
  • E. constipation

Correct answer: A
a. Correct (as the Exception). Nausea is not a typical symptom of fibroids unless a rare acute abdomen occurs.
b. Incorrect. Most common symptom.
c. Incorrect. Caused by posterior pressure.
d. Incorrect. Caused by bladder compression.
e. Incorrect. Caused by rectal compression.

16. Which of the following conditions shows 69 XXY :

  • A. hydrops fetalis
  • B. partial HM

17. Menorrhagia is :

  • A. intermittent irregular vaginal bleeding
  • B. commonly presents as postmenopausal bleeding
  • C. prolonged & heavy menstrual cycle more than 80 ml
  • D. infrequent spaced cycles every 45 days
  • E. the main presentation in case of Asherman syndrome

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect. Describes metrorrhagia.
b. Incorrect. Menorrhagia occurs in pre-menopausal women.
c. Correct. This is the classic clinical definition of heavy menstrual bleeding.
d. Incorrect. Describes oligomenorrhea.
e. Incorrect. Asherman's causes amenorrhea.

18. The following is a symptom of Climacteric (menopause) :

  • A. obesity
  • B. premenstrual tension
  • C. hot flushes
  • D. menorrhagia
  • E. repeated vomiting

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect.
b. Incorrect. PMS stops with cycles.
c. Correct. Vasomotor instability (hot flushes) is the hallmark symptom of estrogen withdrawal.
d. Incorrect. Bleeding stops in menopause.
e. Incorrect.

19. The following drugs are associated with hirsuitism ,except :

  • A. danazol

b. Incorrect. Causes hypertrichosis.
c. Incorrect. Androgenic.
d. Correct (as the Exception). Finasteride is a 5-alpha reductase inhibitor used to treat hirsutism.
e. Incorrect. Causes hypertrichosis.
