Gynecology & Obstetrics Questions

1. Physiology of the menstrual cycle. Identify which of the following statements is incorrect?

  • A. Ovulation follows 36 hours after the LH surge.
  • B. Ovulation is as a result of 'negative feedback' effect on the hypothalamus and pituitary of oestrogen at its maximum.
  • C. The corpus luteum produces progesterone.
  • D. Withdrawal of endogenous progesterone results in endometrial breakdown and menstruation.
  • E. Average cycle length is 27-35 days.

Correct answer: B
a. True. Ovulation typically occurs 34–36 hours after the onset of the LH surge.
b. False. The LH surge is caused by a positive feedback mechanism of high estrogen, not negative feedback.
c. True. The corpus luteum produces progesterone to support the endometrium.
d. True. Progesterone withdrawal triggers menstruation.
e. True. A normal cycle range is typically defined as 21–35 days.

2. The following statements refer to heavy menstrual bleeding (menorrhagia). Which is true?

  • A. Clinically, can be defined as excessive menstrual blood loss that interferes with a woman's physical, emotional, social or material quality of life.
  • B. It is >80 mL/menstrual cycle loss.
  • C. Endometrial and cervical malignancy may be the causes of it.
  • D. Thyroid disease, hemostatic disorders and use of anticoagulant medication should be considered.
  • E. All of the above.

Correct answer: E
a. This is the modern clinical definition (NICE guidelines).
b. This is the objective research definition (>80 mL).
c. Malignancy is a pathological cause.
d. Systemic causes (coagulopathies, hypothyroidism) are recognized etiologies.
e. All statements are factually correct.

3. Uterine fibroid is:

  • A. Benign tumours of the myometrium present in 40% of women.
  • B. Uterine fibroids are more common in Caucasian women,
  • C. Around 10% of fibroids are leiomyosarcomata.
  • D. Family history is present in some cases
  • E. All of the above

Correct answer: A
a. True. Uterine fibroids are the most common benign solid tumors in women.
b. False. They are significantly more common in women of African descent.
c. False. Malignant transformation is rare (less than 1%).
d. True, but A is the best definition/prevalence statistic.

4. Endometrial carcinoma ,all true except:

  • A. Stage 1 disease is confined to the uterus and includes the cervix, lymph nodes but not the vagina.
  • B. Adenocarcinoma of the columnar endometrial gland cells accounts for >90%.
  • C. Risk factors include PCOS, nulliparity, late menopause, tamoxifen use.
  • D. Postmenopausal bleeding is the most common presentation.
  • E. Hysterectomy is involved in the management.

Correct answer: A
a. False. Stage I is confined to the corpus uteri. Involvement of the cervix classifies it as Stage II.
b. True. Endometrioid adenocarcinoma is the most common histology.
c. True. These increase exposure to unopposed estrogen.
d. True. PMB is the hallmark symptom.
e. True. Hysterectomy is the standard surgical management.

5. Regarding the anatomy and function of the cervix:

  • A. The uterosacral ligaments attach the cervix laterally to the pelvic side walls.
  • B. The cardinal ligaments attach the cervix to the posterior pelvic walls.
  • C. The columnar epithelium undergoes metaplasia in some cases.
  • D. The ectocervix is continuous with the vagina and is covered in columnar (glandular) epithelium.
  • E. The endocervix is lined by squamous epithelium.

Correct answer: C
a. False. Uterosacral ligaments attach posteriorly to the sacrum.
b. False. Cardinal ligaments attach laterally to the pelvic side walls.
c. True. Squamous metaplasia occurs at the transformation zone.
d. False. The ectocervix is covered by squamous epithelium.
e. False. The endocervix is lined by columnar epithelium.

6. Meigs' syndrome is:

  • A. Fibromas can cause it.
  • B. Thecoma can cause it.
  • C. Ascites and a right pleural effusion are found in conjunction with the small ovarian mass.
  • D. Removal of the tumor cure the condition.
  • E. All of the above

Correct answer: E
a. True. Ovarian fibroma is the classic cause.
b. True. Other benign solid tumors can cause it.
c. True. The classic triad includes ascites and pleural effusion.
d. True. Resolution of fluid follows tumor removal.

7. Ovarian malignancy:

  • A. Symptoms are usually initially absent and 90% of patients present with stage 3–4 disease.
  • B. The ovary is a common site of metastatic spread, particularly from the breast and GI tract.
  • C. Up to 10% of malignant ovarian masses are secondary tumors.
  • D. The primary malignancy may be difficult to detect and the prognosis is very poor.
  • E. All of the above.

Correct answer: E
a. True. It is often diagnosed late.
b. True. Krukenberg tumors and breast mets are common.
c. True. Metastatic tumors are significant.
d. True. Prognosis is often poor due to late stage.

8. Ovarian tumor markers are all true except:
