Obstetrics & Gynecology Questions

1. The greatest diameter of the fetal head is

  • A. Occipitofrontal
  • B. Occipitomental
  • C. Suboccipit bregmatic
  • D. Bitemporal
  • E. Biparietal

Correct answer: B
b. Occipitomental is 13.5 cm (brow presentation), the largest anteroposterior diameter.
a. Occipitofrontal is 11.5 cm.
c. Suboccipitobregmatic is 9.5 cm.
d. Bitemporal is 8.0 cm.
e. Biparietal is 9.5 cm.

2. What's true : Molding of the fetal head:

  • A. Usually cause brain damage
  • B. Becomes progressively easier as gestational age increase
  • C. Increase the difficulty of delivery
  • D. Does NOT have time to occur in breech delivery
  • E. Does not happen when maternal pelvis is adequate.

Correct answer: D
d. In breech delivery, the aftercoming head passes through the pelvis rapidly, allowing little time for molding.
a. Normal molding is physiological.
b. It becomes harder as ossification increases with age.
c. It decreases difficulty by reducing head diameters.

3. The following are all risk factors for placental abruption EXCEPT:

  • A. Hypertension
  • B. Pre-eclampsia
  • C. Diabetes
  • D. Alcohol
  • E. Tobacco use

Correct answer: C
c. Diabetes is a risk factor for polyhydramnios and macrosomia, but not a primary direct risk factor for abruption compared to vascular disorders.
a, b, d, e. Hypertension, Alcohol, and Tobacco are established risk factors.

4. The following are all common fetal complications of multiple pregnancy EXCEPT

  • A. Preterm labour
  • B. Intrauterine growth retardation
  • C. Malpresentation
  • D. Jaundice
  • E. Shoulder dystocia

Correct answer: E
e. Shoulder dystocia is associated with macrosomia. Twins are usually smaller/IUGR, making shoulder dystocia less common than in singletons.
a, b, c, d. These are very common complications in twins.

5. A multigravida woman presents in labor, by vaginal examination you feel the fontanelle, sinciput and orbital ridges the presentation is:

  • A. Transverse
  • B. Face
  • C. Breech
  • D. Brow
  • E. Cephalic

Correct answer: D
d. Brow presentation is characterized by palpating the anterior fontanelle, sinciput (forehead), and orbital ridges.
b. Face presentation would involve palpating the chin.

6. Uncontrolled hyperthyroidism in pregnancy is associated with all of the following except

  • A. Thyroid storm (thyrotoxic crisis)
  • B. Heart failure.
  • C. Miscarriage
  • D. Hypotension
  • E. Premature labour.

Correct answer: D
d. Hyperthyroidism is associated with hypertension (or widened pulse pressure), not hypotension.
a, b, c, e. These are known complications of the hyperdynamic state caused by hyperthyroidism.

7. Which of the following is not a consequence of massive obstetric haemorrhage

  • A. Acute hypovolaemia
  • B. Hepatic cirrhosis.
  • C. Sudden and rapid cardiovascular decompensation.
  • D. Disseminated intravascular coagulopathy (DIC).
  • E. Pulmonary oedema.

Correct answer: B
b. Hepatic cirrhosis is a chronic disease, not an acute consequence of hemorrhage.
a, c, d. These are direct acute consequences of massive blood loss.
e. Pulmonary edema can occur due to aggressive fluid resuscitation.

8. For cord prolapse management, what's true if baby is dead:

  • A. Vacuum delivery in full cervical dilatation..
  • B. Forceps delivery in previous 3 caesarean
  • C. Internal version in transverse lie.
  • D. Caesarean section in 3 cm dilatation
  • E. Vaginal delivery in cephalic presentation with previous vaginal deliveries

Correct answer: E
e. If the fetus is dead, maternal safety is the priority. Vaginal delivery is preferred to avoid surgical risks.
d. C-section is avoided for a dead fetus unless there is a maternal indication.

9. Diagnosis of cephalopelvic disproportion during labour is:

  • A. There is inefficient uterine contraction.
  • B. Fetal head descend rapidly.
  • C. Thin cervix.
  • D. Progressive excessive molding.
  • E. Liquor changed from meconium to white color

Correct answer: D
d. Progressive excessive molding and caput succedaneum despite contractions indicate a mismatch between head and pelvis (CPD).
b. Rapid descent rules out CPD.

10. Regarding Secondary postpartum haemorrhage:

  • A. diagnosed when bleeding occurs in first 24 hours
  • B. Breast feeding is contraindicated in those patients.
  • C. The commonest cause is cervical tear.
  • D. Choriocarcinoma could be a cause.
  • E. All of the above are true.

Correct answer: D
d. Choriocarcinoma is a rare but possible cause of late postpartum bleeding.
a. Secondary PPH occurs after 24 hours.
c. The commonest cause is retained placental tissue or endometritis.

11. For uterine inversion; what's true?

  • A. Can occur before baby delivery.
  • B. Can occur before placental delivery.
  • C. It is very common.
  • D. Caused by bad first stage of labour management.
  • E. Never be fatal.

Correct answer: B
b. Inversion often occurs before placental delivery or during attempts to deliver it (cord traction).
d. It is caused by mismanagement of the third stage.

12. Initial treatment to arrest preterm labour include all of the following except:

  • A. External cardiotocography (CTG) monitoring.
  • B. Tocolysis.
  • C. Corticosteroids administration.
  • D. Bed rest & hydration.
  • E. Oxytocin administration.

Correct answer: E
e. Oxytocin stimulates contractions and is used for induction, not to arrest labor.
a, b, c, d. These are standard management steps for preterm labor.

13. How long does the uterus takes to become not palpable per abdomen after delivery ?

  • A. One week.
  • B. Two weeks.
  • C. Three weeks.
  • D. Four weeks.
  • E. Six weeks.

Correct answer: B
b. The uterus involutes and descends into the pelvis, becoming non-palpable abdominally by approximately 10-14 days (2 weeks).

14. A perineal tear involving perineal muscles, external and internal anal sphincter and rectal mucosa is categorized as:

  • A. First degree.
  • B. Second degree.
  • C. Third degree.
  • D. Fourth degree.
  • E. None of the above.

Correct answer: D
d. Fourth degree tears involve the anal sphincter complex AND the rectal mucosa.
c. Third degree involves the sphincter but spares the mucosa.

15. In routine chemical tests performed on amniotic fluid, fetal lung maturity can be detected by:

  • A. Creatinine measurement.
  • B. Alpha feto protein.
  • C. Lecithin-sphingomyelin ratio (L/S ratio).
  • D. Bilirubin scan.
  • E. Cytokine.

Correct answer: C
c. The L/S ratio measures surfactant production; a ratio >2.0 indicates maturity.

16. A 38 weeks pregnant primiparous woman in the emergency room. While being examine she has a convulsion. Most likely she has:

  • A. Eclampsia.
  • B. Epilepsy.
  • C. Space occupying lesion.
  • D. Hypoglycemia.
  • E. Hypernatremia.

Correct answer: A
a. In a pregnant woman near term, a new-onset seizure is Eclampsia until proven otherwise.

17. For a pregnant woman with a transverse lie true;

  • A. External version can be done before labour.
  • B. Obstructed labour occur if untreated.
  • C. Rupture uterus may be a complication.
  • D. Cord prolapse may occur.
  • E. All of the above.

Correct answer: E
e. All options are correct: ECV can be attempted, but if labour starts, it leads to obstruction, potential rupture, and high risk of cord prolapse.

18. The following maneuvers are used to manage shoulder dystocia except:

  • A. gentle traction
  • B. MacRoberts' maneuver
  • C. fundal pressure
  • D. Woods' Corkscrew Maneuver
  • E. suprapubic pressure

Correct answer: C
c. Fundal pressure is CONTRAINDICATED in shoulder dystocia as it impacts the shoulder further and risks uterine rupture.
a, b, d, e. These are standard maneuvers for shoulder dystocia.

19. Prerequisites for instrumental delivery include which of the following:

  • A. Contracted pelvis
  • B. 8 cm cervical dilatation
  • C. Ruptured membranes
  • D. Face presentation
  • E. Full bladder

Correct answer: C
c. Ruptured membranes and full dilation (10 cm) are absolute prerequisites.
a. Contracted pelvis is a contraindication.
e. Bladder must be empty.

20. Therapeutic termination of Pregnancy for patients with cardiac disease should be offered in the following conditions except:

  • A. Dilated cardiomyopathy.
  • B. Marfan s Syndrome with dilated aortic root
  • C. Severe Obstructive Lesions .
  • D. Pulmonary hypertension .
  • E. Mitral regurgitation

Correct answer: E
e. Mitral regurgitation is generally well-tolerated in pregnancy. The other conditions carry extremely high maternal mortality risk (WHO Class IV).

21. Which of the following is not true regarding intrauterine growth restriction?

  • A. It is associated with premature labour.
  • B. It may be associated with a low socio-economic status.
  • C. These babies are at an increased risk of developing respiratory distress syndrome.
  • D. It may be associated with raised serum AFP at 16 weeks followed by normal scan at 18 weeks.
  • E. There may be neonatal hypoglycaemia in the babies born with IUGR.

Correct answer: C
c. IUGR fetuses often have accelerated lung maturation due to stress, so they are at a relatively lower risk of RDS compared to AGA preterms (though they have other risks).

22. Which of the following statement is correct regarding the early pregnancy loss?

  • A. Is usually due to hormone deficiency.
  • B. Is due to an abnormal karyotype in approximately 50% of cases.
  • C. Intrauterine pregnancy can not co-exist with tubal pregnancy.
  • D. An intrauterine pregnancy should be visible on transvaginal scan if the HCG is >1000 iu /l.
  • E. Always needs evacuation.

Correct answer: B
b. Chromosomal abnormalities account for 50-60% of first-trimester miscarriages.
d. Discriminatory zone is usually 1500-2000 IU/L.

23. A woman is found to have oligohydramnios at 30/40 . Which of the following is the most likely cause ?

  • A. Duodenal atresia .
  • B. Placental choriangioma.
  • C. Diabetes
  • D. Oesophageal atresia.
  • E. Renal agenesis.

Correct answer: E
e. Renal agenesis leads to decreased urine production, which is the main source of amniotic fluid.
a, b, c, d. These are causes of Polyhydramnios.

24. Which of the following is not an increased risk in multiple pregnancy?

  • A. Placenta praevia.
  • B. Diabetes mellitus.
  • C. Pre-eclampsia.
  • D. Malpresentation.

Correct answer: B
b. While risk increases slightly, Pre-eclampsia, Previa, and Malpresentation are much more significantly and classically associated with multiple pregnancy complications than Diabetes.

25. Which of the drugs is not commonly used in postpartum haemorrhage?

  • A. Mifepristone.
  • B. Misoprostol.
  • C. Oxytocin.
  • D. Ergotamine.
  • E. All of the above.

Correct answer: A
a. Mifepristone is an anti-progestogen used for abortion; it relaxes the uterus and would worsen PPH.
b, c, d. These are uterotonics used to treat PPH.

26. The following complications are more common after instrumental delivery except:

  • A. Anaemia.
  • B. Prolonged perineal discomfort.
  • C. Mastitis.
  • D. Obstetric palsy.
  • E. Puerperal infection.

Correct answer: C
c. Mastitis is related to breastfeeding, not the mode of delivery.
a, b, d, e. Instrumental delivery increases trauma, bleeding, and infection risks.

27. Progress in labour is measured by which of the following :

  • A. The frequency of uterine contractions.
  • B. The force of uterine contractions.
  • C. Descent of the presenting part.
  • D. Cervical length.
  • E. The length of time since rupture of membranes.

Correct answer: C
c. Progress is strictly defined by Cervical Dilation and Descent of the presenting part.

28. Consequences of placental abruption include all except:

  • A. Hypovolaemic shock.
  • B. Fetal distress.
  • C. Amniotic fluid embolus.
  • D. Increased perinatal mortality.
  • E. Intrauterine death.

Correct answer: C
c. Amniotic Fluid Embolus is a separate entity. Shock, fetal distress, and death are direct consequences of abruption.

29. A patient went into shock immediately after normal delivery , likely cause is :

  • A. Postpartum haemorrhage.
  • B. Amniotic fluid embolism.
  • C. Uterine inversion.
  • D. Eclampsia.
  • E. All of the above.

Correct answer: C
c. Neurogenic shock occurring immediately after delivery (often due to cord traction) is characteristic of Uterine Inversion.

30. Indications for induction of labour include:

  • A. Pregnancy induced hypertension.
  • B. Bad obstetric history.
  • C. Diabetes.
  • D. Heart disease.
  • E. All of the above.

Correct answer: E
e. All listed conditions are common indications to induce labor to prevent maternal or fetal complications.

31. Which of the following statement is not correct regarding miscarriage?

  • A. If recurrent, it can be associated with parental chromosomal translocation.
  • B. If recurrent, it can be associated with sickle cell trait.
  • C. The clinical diagnosis of incomplete miscarriage is assisted by digital examination of the cervical canal.
  • D. Cervical incompetence causes recurrent second trimester miscarriage.
  • E. Missed miscarriage should be suspected if the uterine size is smaller than that expected for gestational age.

Correct answer: B
b. Sickle cell trait is not a recognized cause of recurrent miscarriage.

32. Which of the following complication is associated with multiple births?

  • A. Increased incidence of congenital abnormalities.
  • B. Increased incidence of growth retardation.
  • C. Increased incidence of postpartum haemorrhage.
  • D. Decreased incidence of preterm labour.
  • E. All of the above.

Correct answer: D
d. This is the false statement (answer to "Which is NOT true" in context). Multiple births have an Increased incidence of preterm labor.

33. Placenta previa is characterized by all except:

  • A. Painless bleeding.
  • B. Causeless bleeding.
  • C. Recurrent bleeding.
  • D. Presents after 1st trimester.
  • E. High risk / (High head).

Correct answer: E
e. "High risk" is vague and not a specific characteristic of the bleeding itself. The classic triad is Painless, Causeless, Recurrent.

34. All of the following are used for induction of labour ,except :

  • A. PGF2a tablet .
  • B. PGE1 tablet .
  • C. PGE2 gel .
  • D. Misoprostol .
  • E. All of the above .

Correct answer: A
a. PGF2a (Carboprost) is used for PPH, not induction (too toxic/side effects). PGE1 (Misoprostol) and PGE2 (Dinoprostone) are used for induction.

35. Which of the following is not true regarding placental abruption?

  • A. May have no associated vaginal bleeding.
  • B. Is an indication for delivery.
  • C. Has a higher incidence with maternal cocaine abuse.
  • D. May be identified using ultrasound to demonstrate retroplacental clot.
  • E. The diagnosis of concealed abruption can be easily confused with that of acute appendicitis..

Correct answer: D
d. Ultrasound is not sensitive for abruption (<50%); a negative scan does not rule it out. It is a clinical diagnosis.

36. Which of the following is true regarding ABO incompatibility between mother and fetus?

  • A. May affect the first pregnancy.
  • B. Worsens with successive pregnancies.
  • C. Usually causes significant anaemia of the fetus at birth.
  • D. Often requires exchange transfusion.
  • E. Is caused by the Rh(D) antigen.

Correct answer: A
a. ABO incompatibility can affect the first pregnancy because isohemagglutinins exist naturally. It is usually mild.

  • D. Threatened abortion

d. Threatened abortion is the most appropriate term for vaginal bleeding with a closed cervix and viable fetus up to 20-24 weeks.

  • A. Chorioamnionitis.
  • B. Eclampsia.

41. Which is false regarding eclampsia ?

43. In thromboembolism , the risk factors specific to pregnancy include all of the following except :

  • A. Multiple pregnancy.
  • B. Pre eclampsia.
  • C. Smoking.
  • D. Sepsis.
  • E. Grand multiparity.

Correct answer: C
c. Smoking is a general risk factor, but Pre-eclampsia, Multiple Pregnancy, and Sepsis are high-weight "pregnancy-specific" physiological risks for VTE.

44. In breech presentation , which statement is false ?

  • C. The best method of delivering a singleton term breech is by vaginal delivery.
  • D. Multiple gestation is a contraindication to ECV.
  • E. Forceps can be used in the delivery of the head.

Correct answer: C
c. Planned Caesarean Section is generally considered safer for the fetus in singleton term breech than vaginal delivery.

45. All of the following are suggestive of CPD(cephalopelvic disproportion) except :

  • A. Fetal head is not engaged.
  • B. Despite efficient uterine contractions , progress is slow or arrested.
  • C. Head is well applied to the cervix.
  • D. Severe moulding and caput formation.
  • E. Haematuria.

Correct answer: C
c. If the head is well applied to the cervix, it indicates good fit. In CPD, the head remains high and the cervix balloons.

46. Whats true about postpartum haemorrhage?

b. Massive/Major hemorrhage is often categorized as >1500-2500ml. The other options are strictly false (PPH >500ml, Primary is Atony).

47. Late deceleration indicates:

  • A. Head compression.
  • B. Cord compression.

  • D. Breech presentation.
  • E. Normal finding on CTG.

Correct answer: C
c. Late decelerations indicate Uteroplacental Insufficiency leading to fetal hypoxia.

48. In otherwise uncomplicated preterm labour, evidence suggests that the use of tocolytics delays delivery By ?

  • A. 24 hours.

  • D. 7 days.

Correct answer: B
b. Tocolytics are proven to delay delivery by about 48 hours, allowing time for steroid administration.

50. Delivery of the placenta is by:

  • B. Controlled cord traction

  • B. Twin to twin transfusion

  • A. First trimester
  • B. Second trimester
  • C. Third trimester
