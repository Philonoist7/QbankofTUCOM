U1. Regarding OA of the knee joint, all true except one:

  • A. The typical pattern for OA of the knee is bilateral asymmetrical involvement of the medial tibiofemoral compartment
  • B. Best assessed on standing weight-bearing views
  • C. Subchondral cystic change is due to joint fluid herniation into bone through a cartilage defect.
  • D. Periarticular osteopenia
  • E. Subchondral sclerosis and marginal osteophytic changes

Correct answer: D
a. True: Osteoarthritis of the knee often affects both knees (bilateral) but typically does so asymmetrically, with the medial compartment being most commonly involved.
b. True: Standing weight-bearing views are crucial for assessing joint space narrowing accurately in weight-bearing joints like the knee in osteoarthritis.
c. True: Subchondral cysts (geodes) in osteoarthritis are thought to form from synovial fluid being forced into the subchondral bone through defects in the cartilage.
d. False: Periarticular osteopenia (reduced bone density around the joint) is characteristic of inflammatory arthritis like Rheumatoid Arthritis. Osteoarthritis is typically associated with subchondral sclerosis (increased bone density).
e. True: Subchondral sclerosis and the formation of marginal osteophytes are classic radiographic features of osteoarthritis.

U2. Regarding the radiographic features of OA in the Knee & hip, which of the following is true?

  • A. symmetrical joint space narrowing
  • B. periarticular osteopenia
  • C. Best assessed on standing weight-bearing views and bilateral involvement of the knees is common.
  • D. Among the peculiar hip OA features are migration of the femoral head in a supero-lateral direction and less commonly, axial migrating

Correct answer: C
a. False: Joint space narrowing in osteoarthritis is typically asymmetrical, not symmetrical. Symmetrical narrowing is more characteristic of inflammatory arthritis.
b. False: Periarticular osteopenia is a feature of inflammatory conditions like rheumatoid arthritis. Osteoarthritis typically shows sclerosis.
c. True: Standing weight-bearing X-rays are the best way to assess joint space narrowing in weight-bearing joints like the knee. Bilateral knee involvement in OA is also common.
d. False: While supero-lateral migration of the femoral head is a common feature of hip OA, axial migration is more commonly associated with inflammatory arthritis (like RA) rather than being a typical feature of primary OA of the hip. Medial migration can also occur in OA. The statement is potentially misleading in the context of choosing a single best answer.

Bone Tumor

1. Regarding benign bone tumor, all true except:

  • A. Solid or no periosteal reaction.
  • B. Geographic or no cortical destruction.
  • C. Wide zone of transition.
  • D. Malignant bone tumors are much less common than benign bone tumors.

Correct answer: C
a. Solid or no periosteal reaction: Correct. Benign tumors typically have a solid, uninterrupted periosteal reaction or no reaction at all, indicating slow growth.
b. Geographic or no cortical destruction: Correct. Geographic destruction (well-defined, sharply marginated) is characteristic of slow-growing, benign lesions. No cortical destruction can also be seen.
c. Wide zone of transition: Incorrect. A wide zone of transition (ill-defined margin between the lesion and normal bone) is characteristic of aggressive, malignant tumors or infections, not benign tumors which typically have a narrow zone of transition.
d. Malignant bone tumors are much less common than benign bone tumors: Correct. This is a true epidemiological statement; primary benign bone tumors are significantly more common than primary malignant bone tumors.

2. Regarding patterns of bone destruction:

  • A. Lymphoma, leukemia and Ewing's sarcoma showed patterns similar to permeative and moth eaten types.
  • B. Osteomyelitis showed permeative type patterns of bone destruction.
  • C. leukemia showed permeative type .
  • D. moth eaten types represent a probable malignancy

Correct answer: D
a. Lymphoma, leukemia and Ewing's sarcoma showed patterns similar to permeative and moth eaten types: Correct. These conditions are known to cause aggressive bone destruction, which can manifest as permeative or moth-eaten patterns.
b. Osteomyelitis showed permeative type patterns of bone destruction: Correct. Severe or chronic osteomyelitis can mimic malignant tumors and present with a permeative pattern of bone destruction.
c. leukemia showed permeative type: Correct. Leukemia, particularly in children, can infiltrate the bone marrow and cause a permeative pattern of destruction.
d. moth eaten types represent a probable malignancy: Correct. The moth-eaten pattern of bone destruction, characterized by multiple small, irregular lytic areas with poorly defined margins, is highly suggestive of an aggressive process, most commonly a malignancy.

3. Malignant bone tumor all true except:

  • A. Narrow transition zone
  • B. (e.g., Wide transition zone)
  • C. (e.g., Aggressive periosteal reaction)

Correct answer: A
a. Narrow transition zone: Correct (as the "except" answer). A narrow zone of transition is characteristic of benign lesions, not malignant ones, which typically show a wide, ill-defined zone of transition.
b. (e.g., Wide transition zone): Incorrect (as the "except" answer, meaning this statement IS true for malignant tumors). Malignant tumors typically have a wide zone of transition.
c. (e.g., Aggressive periosteal reaction): Incorrect (as the "except" answer, meaning this statement IS true for malignant tumors). Malignant tumors often exhibit aggressive periosteal reactions like Codman's triangle, sunburst, or lamellated (onion-peel) patterns.
d. (e.g., Cortical destruction): Incorrect (as the "except" answer, meaning this statement IS true for malignant tumors). Malignant tumors commonly cause cortical destruction, which can be permeative or moth-eaten.

4. Regarding bone tumors, all are false except:

  • A. Enchondroma is of a benign type
  • B. Osteoclastoma is of a malignant type
  • C. Secondaries (mets) bone tumors are much more common than benign bone tumors.
  • D. Pathological fracture (as a complication)is unique for malignant type

Correct answer: A
a. Enchondroma is of a benign type: Correct. This statement is true. Enchondromas are benign cartilaginous tumors.
b. Osteoclastoma is of a malignant type: Incorrect. This statement is false. Osteoclastoma (Giant Cell Tumor) is typically benign but can be locally aggressive and rarely metastasize or undergo malignant transformation. It's not primarily classified as malignant.
c. Secondaries (mets) bone tumors are much more common than benign bone tumors: Incorrect. This statement is false. While metastatic tumors are the most common malignant tumors in bone, primary benign bone tumors are more common than primary malignant bone tumors.
d. Pathological fracture (as a complication)is unique for malignant type: Incorrect. This statement is false. Pathological fractures can occur in both benign (e.g., simple bone cyst, fibrous dysplasia) and malignant bone tumors due to weakened bone.

6. Regarding the permeative type of bone destruction:

  • A. ill-defined border with narrow transitional zone
  • B. Seen in lymphoma,myeloma..
  • C. spread through bone marrow.

7. Regarding bony tumors,the correct answer is :
