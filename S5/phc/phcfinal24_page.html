Community Medicine and Public Health Questions

1. Puerperium: (all true except one)

  • A. is a period of 42 days post labor.
  • B. breast engorgement is common complication.
  • C. Delivered women should visit PHCC 4times in this period.
  • D. Vitamin A is given.
  • E. puerperal sepsis occurs any time during this 6 weeks

Correct answer: D
a. Correct. Puerperium is the ~6 weeks post-childbirth for reproductive organ recovery.
b. Correct. Breast engorgement is common and can lead to complications if unmanaged.
c. Correct. WHO recommends at least four postnatal visits.
d. Incorrect. Vitamin A is not universally given; recommended where deficiency is a problem.
e. Correct. Puerperal sepsis can occur any time up to 42 days postpartum.

2. one of maternal Health care phases is :

  • A. Postnatal care.
  • B. physical therapy.
  • C. Laboratory services.
  • D. Health and nutrition education.
  • E. Immunization

Correct answer: A
a. Correct. Postnatal care is a distinct phase of maternal health care.
b. Incorrect. Physical therapy is a service, not a phase.
c. Incorrect. Laboratory services are supportive, not a phase.
d. Incorrect. Health education is a component across phases, not a phase.
e. Incorrect. Immunization is an intervention, not a phase.

3. PHC elements are (all true except one):

  • A. health education.
  • B. Prevention and control.
  • C. MCH.
  • D. immunization.
  • E. affordable

Correct answer: E
a. Correct. Health education is a core PHC element.
b. Correct. Prevention and control of endemic diseases is a core PHC element.
c. Correct. MCH, including family planning, is a core PHC element.
d. Correct. Immunization is a core PHC element.
e. Incorrect. Affordability is a principle of PHC, not a content element.

4. Huda a pregnant lady visits PHCC at 5/May/2024 at her 20 weeks of gestation (regular visits):-

  • A. next visit will be at 19/May/2024.
  • B. Tetanus toxoid must not be given in next visit.
  • C. calcium supplementation is very recommended at this time.
  • D. Can feel quickening of fetus.

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect. Visits at 20 weeks are typically every 4 weeks.

c. Incorrect. Calcium is recommended, especially with low dietary intake, but D is more definitively expected.
d. Correct. Quickening (fetal movements) is typically felt around 16-22 weeks.
e. Incorrect. Live attenuated vaccines are contraindicated in pregnancy.

6. ORS sachet contains glucose:

  • A. 4.6 g
  • B. 13.5 g
  • C. 1.5 g
  • D. 2.9 g
  • E. 13.5 mg

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect. Not the standard glucose amount.
b. Correct. Standard WHO ORS contains 13.5g glucose/liter.
c. Incorrect. 1.5g is the amount of Potassium chloride in ORS.
d. Incorrect. 2.9g is the amount of Trisodium citrate in ORS.
e. Incorrect. Unit is grams (g), not milligrams (mg), and 13.5mg is too little.

7. the most widely used anthropometric measurements for the evaluation of nutritional status:

  • A. weight
  • B. height
  • C. mid upper arm circumference
  • D. head circumference
  • E. chest circumference

Correct answer: A
a. Correct. Weight is a fundamental and widely used anthropometric measurement.
b. Incorrect. Height is crucial, but weight is often primary for acute changes.
c. Incorrect. MUAC is used for screening, but weight is more universally applied.
d. Incorrect. Head circumference assesses brain growth in young children.
e. Incorrect. Chest circumference is less commonly used for nutritional assessment.

8. Regarding growth, all true except:

  • A. process of physical maturation
  • B. result in increase in size of organs
  • C. occurs by multiplication of cells
  • D. it is qualitative changes
  • E. it is quantitative changes

Correct answer: D
a. Correct. Growth is an integral part of physical maturation.
b. Correct. Growth results in an increase in tissue and organ size.
c. Correct. Growth occurs by cell multiplication (hyperplasia).
d. Incorrect. Growth involves quantitative changes; qualitative changes are development.
e. Correct. Growth fundamentally involves quantitative changes.

9. Which of the following is not a component of DOTS?

  • B. Self-reporting to health services

a. Incorrect. Case detection by microscopy is a core DOTS component.

c. Incorrect. Government commitment is a crucial DOTS component.
d. Incorrect. Standardized recording and reporting is a key DOTS component.
e. Incorrect. Supervised treatment and patient support is a DOTS cornerstone.

18. Regarding school sick leaves which statement is true:

  • A. Measles 4 days before rash until 4 days after rash appears
  • B. Scarlet fever Onset of fever or rash until 24 hours on antibiotic
  • C. Whooping cough Onset of runny nose until 5 days on antibiotic
  • D. Influenza Onset of symptoms until fever gone
  • E. Bronchiolitis Onset of cough until 12 days

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect. School exclusion for measles is typically 4 days from rash appearance.
b. Correct. For scarlet fever, exclusion is until 24 hours of antibiotic treatment.
c. Incorrect. Pertussis exclusion is 5 days on antibiotics; B is a more standard guideline.
d. Incorrect. Influenza exclusion usually until 24 hours fever-free; B is more standard.
e. Incorrect. No standard fixed 12-day exclusion for bronchiolitis.

28. Infant mortality does not include:

  • A. Early neonatal mortality
  • B. Perinatal mortality
  • C. Post neonatal mortality
  • D. Late neonatal mortality
  • E. mortality under one year

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect. Early neonatal mortality is a component of infant mortality.
b. Correct. Perinatal mortality includes stillbirths, which are not live births, so it's not fully part of infant mortality.
c. Incorrect. Post neonatal mortality is a component of infant mortality.
d. Incorrect. Late neonatal mortality is a component of infant mortality.
e. Incorrect. Infant mortality is, by definition, mortality of live-born children under one year.

30. Which one is true about Immunization:

  • A. Rota virus vaccine not given if child aged more than 8 months.
  • B. Maternal tetanus toxoid vaccine give long life protection to child from tetanus disease.
  • C. Polio vaccine given to child in 3 doses.
  • D. Vitamin A (200 000)IU given with hexa vaccine at 6 month of age.
  • E. DPT are given at 8 years old as booster dose.

Correct answer: A
a. Correct. Rotavirus vaccine series has an upper age limit for completion (typically by 8 months).
b. Incorrect. Maternal TT provides temporary passive immunity, not long-life protection for the child.
c. Incorrect. Polio vaccine primary series involves multiple doses.
d. Incorrect. Vitamin A dose for 6-11 months is 100,000 IU, not 200,000 IU.
e. Incorrect. DPT is not used for 8-year-old boosters; Tdap or Td is used.

31. It is number of death at first year of age divided by number of live births

  • A. perinatal mortality rate
  • B. neonatal mortality rate
  • C. new born mortality rate
  • D. Infant mortality rate
  • E. stillbirth

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect. Perinatal mortality includes stillbirths and early neonatal deaths.
b. Incorrect. Neonatal mortality is deaths in the first 28 days per 1000 live births.
c. Incorrect. "New born mortality rate" is often used interchangeably with neonatal mortality.
d. Correct. Infant mortality rate is deaths under one year per 1000 live births.
e. Incorrect. Stillbirth rate is fetal deaths per 1000 total births.
