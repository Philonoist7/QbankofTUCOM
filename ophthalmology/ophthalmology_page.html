Q0. Lazer is .....

  • A. focused
  • B. not coherent
  • C. parallel
  • D. highly energized

Correct answer: C
a. focused: Lasers can be focused to a very small spot to achieve high power density, but being focused is an application or manipulation of the laser beam, not an inherent property of all laser light as it's generated.
b. not coherent: Incorrect. Coherence (where all light waves are in phase in time and space) is a fundamental and defining property of laser light, distinguishing it from ordinary light sources.
c. parallel: Correct. Laser light is highly collimated, meaning the light rays are nearly parallel, resulting in a beam that spreads very little (low divergence) over distance. This is a key characteristic.
d. highly energized: While lasers are sources of amplified light and can produce high-energy beams, "highly energized" is a relative term and not as uniquely defining as collimation or coherence. Other light sources can also be highly energized. However, lasers are indeed a means to concentrate energy. Between C and D, "parallel" describes a more fundamental optical property of the beam's nature.

  • D. photodestruction

  • B. Photocoagulation
  • C. Photoablation
  • D. Ionizing effect

Correct answer: B
a. Photoradiation: This is a very general term referring to the emission of light or radiation. It's not a specific tissue effect.
b. Photocoagulation: Correct. This is a primary thermal effect where laser light is absorbed by tissue, converted to heat, and causes proteins to denature and coagulate (e.g., Argon laser in retinal treatments).
