Community Medicine Questions

1. Regarding hypertensive states of pregnancy which statement is true:

  • A. Transient HTN when BP more than 140/90 with proteinuria or end-organ damage
  • B. Responsible for about 98,000 deaths/year
  • C. Eclampsia affects about 2% pregnancies
  • D. Superimposed preeclampsia on patients with chronic HTN in about 50% of cases
  • E. Maternal mortality rate of eclampsia is 8-36%

Correct answer: B
a. Incorrect. Transient HTN is defined as blood pressure ≥140/90 mmHg after 20 weeks of gestation without proteinuria or signs of end-organ damage.
b. Correct. Hypertensive disorders of pregnancy are a leading cause of maternal mortality; this figure aligns with some global estimates.
c. Incorrect. Eclampsia affects about 0.1-0.5% of pregnancies, not 2%.
d. Incorrect. Superimposed preeclampsia develops in about 10-25% of women with chronic hypertension, not 50%.
e. Incorrect. While mortality can be high, 8-36% is a very wide range, and the upper limit is exceptionally high for most data.

2. Regarding the sick leaves for school children which statement is true:

  • A. In case of TB it is for 2weeks
  • B. It is for 20 days in child with mumps
  • C. It is for 30 days in case of hepatitis A
  • D. In child with measles for 2weeks after occurrence of symptoms
  • E. Child with meningitis should have at least 3 weeks

Correct answer: E
a. Incorrect. For infectious TB, exclusion is until deemed non-infectious, often 2-4 weeks of treatment; "for 2 weeks" is an initial period, not definitive.
b. Incorrect. For mumps, exclusion is typically for 5 days after parotid swelling onset.
c. Incorrect. For hepatitis A, exclusion is generally 1 week after jaundice onset or 2 weeks after symptom onset if no jaundice.
d. Incorrect. For measles, exclusion is typically 4 days after rash onset.
e. Correct. Meningitis often requires prolonged recovery; "at least 3 weeks" is reasonable.

3. In mastitis which statement is true:

  • A. Caused by poor drainage
  • B. Incorrect positioning is unrelated factor
  • C. Treated by nursing on affected side
  • D. Antibiotics is not indicated
  • E. Feed pumping on the affected side is useless

Correct answer: A
a. Correct. Poor milk drainage (milk stasis) is a primary predisposing factor for mastitis.
b. Incorrect. Incorrect positioning and latch are risk factors for mastitis.
c. Incorrect (as primary true statement). While continuing nursing is part of treatment, poor drainage is the primary cause.
d. Incorrect. Antibiotics are often indicated for infective mastitis.
e. Incorrect. Pumping can be useful to ensure complete breast drainage.

5. Which of the following is not true regarding educational approach in health education:

Correct answer: B

6. Regarding tetanus all true except:

  • A. is an bacterial infection
  • B. is a chronic disease
  • C. caused by an exotoxin
  • D. neonatal infection occurs through the exposure of the unhealed umbilical cord stump to tetanus spores
  • E. cause painful muscle spasm

Correct answer: B
a. True. Tetanus is caused by Clostridium tetani.
b. False (this is the answer to "all true except"). Tetanus is an acute disease, not chronic.
c. True. Symptoms are caused by tetanospasmin, an exotoxin.
d. True. This is how neonatal tetanus typically occurs.
e. True. Painful muscle spasms are characteristic.

10. One of the danger signs for referring child with diarrhea to the hospital:

  • A. un able to drink
  • B. thirsty
  • C. sunken eyes
  • D. irritable
  • E. diarrhea

Correct answer: A
a. Correct. Inability to drink or breastfeed is an IMCI danger sign requiring urgent referral.
b. Incorrect. Thirst is a sign of "some dehydration," not a primary danger sign for immediate referral alone.
c. Incorrect. Sunken eyes indicate "some" or "severe dehydration"; "unable to drink" is a more critical general danger sign.
d. Incorrect. Irritability is a sign of "some dehydration." Lethargy is a danger sign.
e. Incorrect. Diarrhea is the condition, not a specific danger sign unless qualified.

11. Which of the following symptoms is not characteristic for pneumonia?

  • A. Productive coughing/expectoration
  • B. Dyspnea
  • C. Decreased respiratory rate
  • D. Fever
  • E. Nausea, vomiting or diarrhea

Correct answer: C
a. Incorrect. Productive cough can be characteristic of pneumonia.
b. Incorrect. Dyspnea (difficulty breathing) is characteristic.
c. Correct (this is 'not characteristic'). Pneumonia typically causes increased respiratory rate (tachypnea).
d. Incorrect. Fever is a common characteristic.
e. Incorrect. These can occur with pneumonia as systemic symptoms, particularly in children.

12. The female sterilization method achieved by surgically cutting and cauterizing the fallopian tubes is

  • A. fallopian cryogenics
  • B. vasectomy
  • C. tubal bypass
  • D. tubal ligation
  • E. Hysterectomy

Correct answer: D
a. Incorrect. Cryogenics (freezing) is not standard for tubal sterilization.
b. Incorrect. Vasectomy is male sterilization.
c. Incorrect. Tubal bypass aims to restore fertility.
d. Correct. Tubal ligation is female sterilization; cutting and cauterizing is a common method.
e. Incorrect. Hysterectomy results in sterility but is not primarily for sterilization.

20. A pregnant women visit PHCC. Her LMP was in 13/December/2018:

  • A. gestational age at 20/June/2019 is 27 weeks.
  • B. EDD is 5/ August/2019.
  • C. vaginal bleeding at this gestational age is threatened abortion.
  • D. Pregnant can visit PHCC each 2 weeks.
  • E. Iron supplementation should be avoided at this time .

Correct answer: A
a. Correct. Calculation: (31-13 Dec) + 31 Jan + 28 Feb + 31 Mar + 30 Apr + 31 May + 20 Jun = 189 days; 189/7 = 27 weeks.
b. Incorrect. EDD (Naegele's rule) is 20/September/2019.
c. Incorrect. Bleeding at 27 weeks is antepartum hemorrhage, not threatened abortion.
d. Incorrect. At 27 weeks, visits are usually every 4 weeks or starting to be every 2 weeks (around 28 weeks).
e. Incorrect. Iron supplementation is recommended in the second and third trimesters.
