Anesthesiology Questions G8

1. regarding postdural puncture headach,all true except:

  • A. caffein is useful line in management in a dose of 500 mg per day.
  • B. it occur mainly in epidural anesthesia.
  • C. epidural blood patch is last and very useful line in management.
  • D. it is closely related to the bore of the needle used.
  • E. usually aggravated by sitting position and relieved by supine position.

Correct answer: B
a. True: Caffeine can be used in PDPH management, and 500mg is a recognized dose.
b. False: PDPH occurs after dural puncture (spinal anesthesia or accidental in epidural), not mainly in epidural anesthesia itself.
c. True: Epidural blood patch is a highly effective treatment for severe PDPH.
d. True: Larger needle bore increases PDPH risk.
e. True: PDPH is classically orthostatic (worse upright, better supine).

  • D. we can extend the duration of epidural anesthesia by use epidural catheter.

Correct answer: D
a. False: Thoracic epidural is more controllable for cholecystectomy analgesia; spinal alone is less common for this.
b. False: Spinal anesthesia uses much smaller volumes of local anesthetic than epidural.
c. False: Apgar scores are generally better or comparable with regional anesthesia (spinal/epidural) for C-section vs. GA.
d. True: Epidural catheters allow continuous or intermittent dosing, extending anesthesia duration.
e. False: Cost-effectiveness is complex; neuraxial is not definitively more costly or demanding in all aspects than GA.

3. regarding ketamine, all true except:

  • B. suitable agent for brain surgery.
  • C. good choice for asthmatic patient.
  • D. avoided in epileptic patient.

Correct answer: B
a. True: Ketamine has strong analgesic effects.
b. False: Ketamine can increase ICP, making it generally unsuitable for many brain surgeries without specific considerations.
c. True: Ketamine is a bronchodilator, beneficial for asthmatics.
d. True: Ketamine can lower seizure threshold and is generally avoided in epileptics.
e. True: Psychomimetic effects of ketamine can delay or complicate recovery.

d. True: Muscle relaxants facilitate endotracheal intubation.
e. True: Muscle relaxants are not universally used in all anesthesia types.

e. True: Nausea and vomiting are common side effects of opioids.
